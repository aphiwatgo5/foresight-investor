Seeking Alpha Quant

What it is: A quantitative stock-rating system from Seeking Alpha that scores equities on five factor grades and rolls them into an overall rating (Strong Buy → Strong Sell). In MDAEM v3.4 it is the external calibration anchor and a hard gate.

Summary

SA Quant produces letter grades (A+ to F) across five factors and a composite rating. MDAEM uses it two ways: (1) as Hard Gate 6 — a Sell or Strong Sell is an automatic reject — and (2) as a sizing overlay that caps MDAEM's own tier-based sizing, with the explicit rule that when MDAEM and SA conflict, take the lower signal. MDAEM also calibrates its own factor scoring against SA grades, tracking a "match rate" and "factor accuracy" over successive rounds (~75–85% in 2026; target <10% factor error).

Key facts

  • Five factor grades used by MDAEM: Valuation · Growth · Profitability · Momentum · Revisions (mdaem-v34-framework).
  • The Revisions factor (EPS up/down revision counts) feeds MDAEM's Ref L hard gate: up:down ratio <0.2 → REJECT (mdaem-v34-framework).
  • Overall rating buckets: Strong Buy / Buy / Hold / Sell / Strong Sell.
  • MDAEM treats a Hold as a sizing cap, not a disqualifier; Sell/Strong Sell disqualifies.

Relationships

  • Central input to mdaem-framework (gate 6 + overlay + calibration target).
  • Appears throughout the mdaem-validation-database entries (e.g., DDD upgraded to Strong Buy; QBTS/RGTI/IBM Hold; BABA Revisions 0:28).

Notes & tensions

  • It is a third-party black-box factor model; MDAEM uses it as a check and disconfirming signal, not as gospel — the framework rejected several SA Strong Buy names (ONDS, TNGX) on its own asymmetry gate (Ref 19).
  • Distinct from this vault's own prediction-ledger and trend-radar conviction scores, which are internally derived and insight-only.
