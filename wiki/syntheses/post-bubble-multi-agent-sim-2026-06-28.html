Post-Bubble Multi-Agent Simulation — what happens after, and when

Bottom line (the emergent forecast): An agent-based simulation of the seven actors who drive the
AI-equity bubble produces a strikingly consistent three-act sequence, not a single crash:
- Act 1 — Euphoria completes first (now → ~2026 H2/Q4). Six of seven agents say there is no
near-term crash: sentiment is still fearful (bubble-risk-us-equity-2026-06-28, F&G 25), so the
bubble most likely gets bigger — a melt-up to new highs as the missing greed-flip arrives.
- Act 2 — The reflexive break (~2027, esp. H2-2027 → 2028). The trigger is unanimous: a
hyperscaler AI-capex guide-down + disappointing AI ROIC breaks the Soros loop → HY credit
spreads widen first → vendor financing unwinds → margin cascade → a sharp 25–40% drawdown.
- Act 3 — The long de-rate / rotation (2027–2029+). Not a V-shaped recovery: a multi-year
"lost decade" de-rating of US-megacap concentration while capital rotates to ex-US / gold /
real assets — the macro-regime "end of US exceptionalism" theme, realized.

This is a scenario simulation — inference, not data. Reflexive systems are intrinsically
unpredictable and no framework reliably times tops. Outputs are falsifiable scenario calls with
probabilities + invalidation, logged in prediction-ledger. Insight/research only — not advice.

3. Emergent dynamics (the "deep interaction" read)

  • Convergence on sequencing. Independently, 6/7 agents reject a near-term crash and expect the
    euphoria to complete first — because the marginal buyer (fearful, still-levered retail) isn't
    exhausted and capex is still validating prices. The most dangerous near-term outcome is the bubble
    getting bigger.
  • The trigger chain is unanimous and ordered: hyperscaler capex guide-down (demand) → AI
    ROIC disappointment     (breaks the Soros loop) → HY spreads widen (credit cracks first) →
    vendor-financing unwind + margin cascade (retail forced out on a fast drop) → drawdown.
  • No early rescue. The Fed's put is on plumbing, not equities — it activates only at ~30%+
    drawdown / funding stress, so nothing cushions Act 2 until it's already deep.
  • Crash vs rotation is a sequence, not a disagreement. The "sharp 25–50% drawdown" camp
    (CFO/semis/retail/short) describes Act 2; the "multi-year rotation/de-rate" camp (CIO/Fed)
    describes Act 3. Both are in the base case, in order.

7. Caveats

  • Simulation = inference, not data. Agent personas are reasoning from incentives, not observed behaviour.
  • Reflexive systems are path-dependent and unpredictable; the timing is a distribution, not a date. Exogenous shocks (geopolitics, an AI breakthrough, a policy error) dominate any model.
  • The base case explicitly says the near-term risk is upside (melt-up), which is harder psychologically than calling a crash.
