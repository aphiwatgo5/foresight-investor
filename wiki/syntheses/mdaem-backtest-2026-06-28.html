5. Calibration — what to tune (v3.5 candidates)

Keep — robustly validated (no change):
- Asymmetry gate (Ref 19) — RKLB/PL crushed; basket alpha ~6.5 pts.
- Extreme-valuation gate (Ref 20, >50× sector) — QBTS −23%, RGTI −31%.
- M&A ceiling (Ref 17) — KALV closed at exactly $27, delisted.
- Revisions / target-below-price (Ref 19) — TEVA flat-down; NVDA/AVGO skip correct.
- Broken-thesis ETF reject (H9) — HYDR −33%; space ETFs −10/−25% (H10).

Tune — the leaks (all are the framework's own pre-flagged bias: "reflexive small-cap dismissal /
late-cycle too cautious"):
1. High-momentum rejects → add a "monitor, don't hard-reject" tier. TNGX +55% is the canonical
miss (Section 9 pre-registered it). Precision is great; recall on momentum continuation is poor.
Consider a tracking/partial sleeve for asymmetry-fails that still carry Revisions A/Strong-Buy.
2. "Wait for pullback" on megatrends → allow a starter tranche. NBIS +12%, TEM +22%, CRDO +9%
never pulled back. A small starter beats a full defer when conviction is high and trend intact.
3. Pre-catalyst financing (Ref 21) — keep, but it's a timing gate. VKTX +23% this window; the
dilution risk is real but lies in 2027 — the gate may still be right by 2026-08-24/2027.
4. China contrarian — require a turn, not just a low. Gate 52w-low entries behind a Revisions or
3-month-momentum inflection (BYD/Xiaomi/PDD fell further from "cheap").

Meta-read: the framework is a strong loss-avoider (the gates that say no are accurate) and a
weaker opportunity-capturer (the rules that say wait/defer cost upside in an uptrend). In a
down tape (this window) that profile shines; in a rip-higher tape it would lag. Re-score at the
2026-08-24 review when earnings (DDD H2, China Q2) and the 3-month criteria mature.
