Top-5 Themes — Opportunity, Threats, Winner & Framework Read

Selection logic. Filtered the 30-theme trend-radar through the
bubble-risk-us-equity-2026-06-28 lens (see bubble-implications-by-theme): favour conviction-4
themes that are either non-discretionary AI-levered or genuine low-correlation diversifiers, and
that survive entry discipline (mdaem-framework / dual-model-shortlist-scorecard-2026-06-03).
That yields Cybersecurity · Power-grid · Defense · GLP-1 · Water. All five are conviction 4; each
diversifies the AI-capex reflexive core in a different way.

Reading the recommendation block. Bubble signal = exposure to the de-rating (from the 7
frameworks). Entry read = MDAEM-style gate/asymmetry + any dual-model verdict. Conviction (1–5,
risk-adjusted, nothing = 5) + an explicit invalidation. Insight & research only — not buy/sell
advice or position sizing. "Winner" = best-positioned competitively, not a recommendation to buy it.

1. cybersecurity — the defensive way to stay AI-levered

Why top-5: the most non-discretionary demand in the compute stack (low-teens growth
regardless of macro) — AI-levered upside with the least cyclicality.

Opportunity
- Near-non-discretionary spend; AI both raises attacker capability and creates new security ARR (net demand-positive).
- Platform consolidation lets leaders cross-sell a widening stack and take share from point products.
- Best feature of any AI-adjacent theme: demand persists in a downturn.

Threats
- Valuation extremes — CrowdStrike ~20.6× sales, Cloudflare ~27.6× EV/Sales: priced for perfection; any slip or self-inflicted outage de-rates hard.
- The 2026 rally may be a supply-consolidation (M&A-multiple) story, not pure demand → mean-reversion risk.
- AI could commoditize some detection layers; point-product names (Okta, SentinelOne, Check Point) get squeezed.

Suggested winner → Palo Alto Networks (PANW). Broadest platform (platformization + CyberArk
identity pillar = widest stack); the consolidator that gains from the trend. CrowdStrike is the
endpoint leader but dual-model REJECT on valuation asymmetry; Zscaler is the zero-trust pure-play.

Research aids: track platform net-new ARR & module attach (PANW), NRR (CRWD/ZS), M&A multiples
(consolidation vs demand); ETF CIBR/BUG; prediction P3 (prediction-ledger). Page: cybersecurity.

Framework recommendation — Bubble signal: 🟢→🔴 AI-levered but defensive. Entry read: theme
strong; the leader (CRWD) fails entry on asymmetry → prefer the basket (CIBR) or PANW over the
priciest single names. Conviction 4. Invalidation: a quarter showing demand (not just M&A
multiples) decelerating to high-single-digits, or a CRWD/PANW NRR break below ~110%.

2. power-grid-electrification — "AI needs power," but on real contracts

Why top-5: the cleanest second-derivative of AI — but expressed through multi-year backlogs and
hyperscaler PPAs, which are contracts, not reflexive multiples.

Opportunity
- Gas-turbine + grid-equipment scarcity → multi-year pricing power (GEV ~100 GW turbine backlog, $163B total backlog).
- Direct AI-DC demand: Eaton DC orders +240%, Electrical Americas backlog +44%; Vertiv FY25 +29% Q3.
- Constellation/Vistra monetize scarce 24/7 clean power via hyperscaler PPAs — visible cash flows.

Threats
- Correlated with the AI-capex complex — a data-center demand re-rate hits it with ai-infrastructure (theme-linkage-map).
- "Air pocket" if hyperscaler power deals get cancelled/renegotiated; supply-chain easing compresses equipment pricing power.
- Valuation: GEV ~$280B prices in a lot; its Q1'26 margin was flattered by a $4.5B Prolec M&A gain (underlying adj EBITDA ~9.6%).

Suggested winner → GE Vernova (GEV) on competitive position (turbine scarcity, orders +86% organic,
book-to-bill ~2.5). But framework caveat: GEV is a dual-model REJECT (Ref-L revisions 0.14) and the
flattered margin → for entry, the GRID basket or Eaton/Vistra are the more disciplined expressions.

Research aids: track gas-turbine backlog & cancellations, Eaton/VRT DC order growth, PPA
announcements (prediction P11 = a new ≥1 GW DC PPA in 2026); ETF GRID/XLU. Page: power-grid-electrification.

Framework recommendation — Bubble signal: 🟠 Correlated (but contract-backed = lower reflexivity
than chips). Entry read: theme conviction high; winner fails dual-model on revisions → express via
basket/Eaton, not GEV at $280B. Conviction 4. Invalidation: a wave of PPA cancellations or
hyperscaler capex-guidance cut (breaks the demand) → would also trip bubble-risk-us-equity-2026-06-28 watch items.

3. defense-security — the genuine diversifier

Why top-5: low correlation to the AI-capex cycle + a structural, policy-backed budget
supercycle = the cleanest portfolio diversifier on the radar.

Opportunity
- NATO 5%-of-GDP pledge + munitions reload cycle = multi-year, visible, policy-backed demand.
- Primes (RTX, Lockheed, Northrop, GD) have deep moats and modest valuations vs the AI names.
- European rearmament: Rheinmetall compounding 30%+ on artillery/ammo scarcity.

Threats
- Geopolitical de-escalation (durable Ukraine/ME peace) would slow the reload cycle.
- Budget politics (US CR/sequestration; European fiscal limits) cap realization.
- Program execution / cost overruns; defense-tech valuations (Palantir ~40–50× sales) are extreme.

Suggested winner → RTX (missiles/air-defense reload, backlog, modest multiple). Dual-model
STARTER (cap ~3%, SA Hold) — passes entry as a starter, not a max-conviction add. Rheinmetall is the
higher-growth EU expression; avoid Palantir on valuation (most multiple-compression risk).

Research aids: track NATO budget prints (prediction P12 = SIPRI 2025 global mil-spend >$2.72T,
resolves 2026-06-30), munitions order flow, US appropriations; ETF ITA/PPA/SHLD. Page: defense-security.

Framework recommendation — Bubble signal: 🟢 Diversifier (this is what you want if the AI loop
reverses). Entry read: RTX STARTER (capped); priced-in but not bubble-priced. Conviction 4.
Invalidation: a credible, durable multi-front peace and a US budget sequestration that stalls
the reload — both together.

4. glp1-obesity-metabolic — the cleanest non-AI growth

Why top-5: a durable demographic growth engine independent of the AI cycle, with a clear
single winner that both engines endorse.

Opportunity
- Obesity TAM ~$95–200B by 2030; oral inflection (orforglipron) expands the addressable market beyond injectables.
- Lilly leads on efficacy (tirzepatide head-to-head), pipeline (retatrutide triple-agonist Ph3), and share (~60%).
- Q1'26 incretin growth +55% with a guidance raise — demand inflection is live.

Threats
- Pricing/reimbursement compression — Medicare negotiation (IRA), payer pushback, cheap orals could cut net pricing faster than volume grows.
- Competitive erosion post-2027 (next-gen orals/multi-agonists fragment the duopoly).
- Tolerability/long-term safety at population scale; compounded/grey-market substitution.

Suggested winner → Eli Lilly (LLY). "Clearly winning" — efficacy + first unrestricted oral +
highest share. Dual-model ADD (MDAEM 74 MED-HIGH, SA Buy) — the rare name where the thematic and
entry frameworks agree. (Novo is the share-loser; Viking the cleanest M&A target.)

Research aids: track tirzepatide/orforglipron scripts & share, CagriSema US decision (Q4'26),
Medicare-negotiation headlines; prediction P8 (LLY incretin ≥+30% YoY FY2026). Page: glp1-obesity-metabolic.

Framework recommendation — Bubble signal: 🟢 Diversifier (demographic, not AI-capex). Entry read:
LLY dual-model ADD — passes gates, asymmetry favourable. Conviction 4. Invalidation: a
materially negative CagriSema/orforglipron safety or efficacy readout, or an IRA pricing outcome that
breaks net-price assumptions.

5. water-resource-scarcity — defensive compounder + AI kicker

Why top-5: the rare theme that is both an all-weather defensive and AI-linked growth — a
shock-absorber if the bubble de-rates, with a real demand tailwind if it doesn't.

Opportunity
- Regulated utilities (AWK) = inelastic demand, monopoly moats, steady rate-base compounding — classic all-weather quality.
- AI-datacenter cooling-water wave (~2.97T liters in 2026, ~11.84% CAGR) layered onto an already-scarce resource — links to data-center-cooling / ai-infrastructure.
- Lowest drawdown-beta of the top-5; behaves defensively when risk assets fall.

Threats
- Interest-rate sensitivity — regulated-utility valuations compress when rates rise (macro-regime; 10Y at 4.38%).
- Regulatory/rate-case risk (allowed returns cut); quality names trade at premium multiples.
- DC water-demand backlash (local opposition) — though that accelerates closed-loop data-center-cooling.

Suggested winner → American Water Works (AWK) as the purest regulated compounder (dual-model
STARTER — defensive contrarian); Xylem (XYL)/Veralto are the best ways to play the
tech/efficiency + datacenter-cooling angle.

Research aids: track rate-case outcomes & allowed ROE, DC water-use regulation, the 10Y (rate
sensitivity); ETF PHO/FIW/CGW (PHO = Tier-1 idea-rank). Page: water-resource-scarcity.

Framework recommendation — Bubble signal: 🟢 Diversifier + defensive ballast. Entry read: AWK
STARTER (defensive); main gate is the rate regime, not company quality. Conviction 4. Invalidation:
a sustained move higher in long rates (10Y >~5%) that compresses utility multiples, or broad
DC water-use restrictions that cap the growth kicker.

Cross-read & cautions

  • Diversification within the five: cyber + power are AI-levered (correlated to each other);
    defense + GLP-1 + water are the true diversifiers. A bubble-aware tilt overweights the latter three.
  • Entry discipline flags the "winner ≠ buy" gap twice: the competitive winners in cyber (CRWD) and
    power (GEV) are dual-model rejects — own the theme via the basket or the disciplined name, not the
    priciest leader. This is the bubble-risk-us-equity-2026-06-28 lesson in microcosm.
