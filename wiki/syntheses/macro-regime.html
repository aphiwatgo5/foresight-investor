Macro Regime

Bottom line: The cross-cutting overlay that conditions every theme on the trend-radar.
Themes don't trade in a vacuum — rates, the dollar, inflation, liquidity, and policy decide which
clusters lead and which de-rate (see theme-linkage-map). As of mid-2026 the regime is one of
moderating-but-sticky inflation, a weakening dollar, still-elevated real rates, and a live
"end-of-US-exceptionalism" rotation — a backdrop that favors real assets, ex-US equities, and
diversification away from the crowded AI-capex complex. Insight only — not advice. Figures are
research-aggregated ~2026; verify live levels before use.

2. How each axis routes to the themes

Rates ↑ (or higher-for-longer) hurts:
- Long-duration tech / us-megacap-quality (multiple compression at ~27–28x fwd)
- Rate-sensitive real assets: healthcare-aging-services (senior-housing REITs/Welltower),
water-resource-scarcity (regulated utilities), renewables-storage (project economics)
- EM flows: india-em-growth, china-contrarian, asean-thailand (USD/rate-driven outflows)
- Non-cash-generative commodities-gold-real-assets (higher opportunity cost)

Rates ↓ / easing helps: all of the above reverse; gold and EM tend to lead.

Weak USD (the current tilt) helps:
- Ex-US equities: india-em-growth, japan-corporate-reform (but erodes unhedged JPY
returns — hedged-vs-unhedged is the key choice), china-contrarian, asean-thailand
- commodities-gold-real-assets (gold inversely tracks USD) and critical-minerals
- The "US exceptionalism" rotation away from us-megacap-quality

Sticky inflation / debasement fear helps:
- commodities-gold-real-assets (the core debasement hedge — central-bank buying ~800t/yr)
- Real assets, infrastructure-capex, natural-gas-lng / critical-minerals (real inputs)
- Hurts long-duration growth multiples if it forces rates higher

Risk-off / liquidity withdrawal hurts (most→least):
- Speculative cluster: space-economy, quantum-computing, tokenization-digital-assets,
clinical-stage biotech-genomics (funding-window-dependent)
- Then the AI-capex complex (ai-infrastructure et al. — see theme-linkage-map cluster 1)
- Defensive ballast holds up: defense-security, water-resource-scarcity,
healthcare-aging-services, commodities-gold-real-assets

Policy / fiscal / protectionism:
- Tailwind: defense-security (NATO 5%-GDP), infrastructure-capex (IIJA), reshoring-supply-chain
+ semiconductors (CHIPS), nuclear-uranium (DPA/grid security), glp1-obesity-metabolic
(offset by 2027 Medicare price negotiation)
- Headwind: us-megacap-quality (antitrust), china-contrarian (US-China tech restrictions),
renewables-storage (IRA-rollback risk)

3. The defining 2026 macro question

Is 2025 the start of a regime change away from US-megacap leadership? The evidence: MSCI World
ex-US +31.8% vs S&P +17.7% in 2025, a −9.5% dollar, gold +75% (2025) into a January-2026 record,
and EM/Japan re-rating. If this persists, the radar tilts toward india-em-growth,
japan-corporate-reform, commodities-gold-real-assets and away from concentrated
us-megacap-quality + the crowded AI-capex complex. If it's a pause, US megacap + AI resume
leadership. This is the single macro switch that most reorders the radar.
