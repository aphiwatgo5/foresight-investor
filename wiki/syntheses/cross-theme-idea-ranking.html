Cross-theme idea ranking — risk-adjusted shortlist

Bottom line: A synthesis across all 28 trend-radar themes of the instruments that screen most attractive on a risk-adjusted basis — with the bull thesis, what confirms it, and what breaks it. Insight only — not buy/sell advice or sizing. The gate-checks, scenario math, and position sizing are MDAEM's job (run by Aphiwat); this page only ranks research attractiveness. Built on the 2026-06-01 analyst-grade scan; figures sourced on each theme page.

The cross-cutting caveat (read first)

The radar's highest-conviction themes — the five conviction-4 AI-stack themes plus power/gas/cooling — all share one demand engine: AI capex (see theme-linkage-map). High trend conviction ≠ attractive entry, and they would de-rate together in an AI-capex air-pocket. So "most attractive" deliberately weights toward (a) the best risk-adjusted name within the AI complex and (b) genuine diversifiers that don't move with it. Nothing earns conviction 5 (risk-adjusted).

Tier 2 — High-quality but entry-sensitive ("wait for the pullback")

  • INDA / SMIN — india-em-growth (4): cleanest multi-decade EM grower, but 2026 is an entry debate. Watch FPI flows turning net-positive + valuation reset. Breaks if GDP sustainably <5% or a bank asset-quality blowup.

Tier 3 — Avoid / lottery-ticket only

  • quantum-computing (2): pure-plays at 100–800× P/S — MDAEM Ref 20 hard-rejects >50× sector (QBTS/RGTI already rejected). Big-tech (IBM/GOOGL) is the only sane exposure.
  • space-economy (2.5): early/binary/cash-burning; the SPCX IPO valuation already fails MDAEM's asymmetry gate.
  • Thailand standalone (~2 within asean-thailand): regional laggard; the ASEAN case is Vietnam/Indonesia, not the SET.

The synthesis

On a risk-adjusted read, the research points away from piling further into the crowded AI-compute core and toward (a) the most defensive AI-levered names (cybersecurity, power, water) and (b) true diversifiers (defense, Japan, gold) — with India and semis as quality names to buy on weakness, not at the highs.
