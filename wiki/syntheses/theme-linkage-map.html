Theme Linkage Map

Bottom line: The ~28 trend-radar themes are not independent bets — they are nodes in a
dependency network driven by a handful of shared forces. This map traces the upstream→downstream
chains and, crucially, the shared-driver clusters that make supposedly-diversified themes move
together. The single most important takeaway: much of the radar is one trade — the AI-capex
trade — wearing several costumes. Insight only — not advice.

1. The master chain — "AI needs power needs metal"

The spine of the entire radar. Each arrow is a real demand-transmission link:

AI compute demand ([ai-infrastructure](ai-infrastructure.html))
   → AI accelerators / foundry ([semiconductors](semiconductors.html))
       → semicap equipment + back-end (→ [reshoring-supply-chain](reshoring-supply-chain.html), Tokyo Electron in [japan-corporate-reform](japan-corporate-reform.html))
   → AI back-end bandwidth ([networking-optical](networking-optical.html))
   → monetization layer ([ai-software-agents](ai-software-agents.html))
   → data-center electricity load ([power-grid-electrification](power-grid-electrification.html))
       → baseload generation ([natural-gas-lng](natural-gas-lng.html), [nuclear-uranium](nuclear-uranium.html), [renewables-storage](renewables-storage.html))
       → thermal management ([data-center-cooling](data-center-cooling.html))
           → cooling water ([water-resource-scarcity](water-resource-scarcity.html))
       → grid build-out copper/transformers ([critical-minerals](critical-minerals.html), [infrastructure-capex](infrastructure-capex.html))
   → security of the whole stack ([cybersecurity](cybersecurity.html))

Why it matters: a demand impulse (or air-pocket) at the top propagates down the entire chain.
The hyperscaler capex line is the master valve. This is the radar's central correlated-de-rating
risk.

2. Secondary chains

  • Reindustrialization: geopolitics/fiscal → defense-security → reshoring-supply-chain
    → automation-robotics → infrastructure-capex → critical-minerals. Defense is the
    least AI-correlated node here (diversifier).
  • Electrification of transport/industry: critical-minerals (copper, lithium, rare earths)
    → renewables-storage (batteries) → EV demand (BYD in china-contrarian) →
    power-grid-electrification.
  • Health demographics: aging (healthcare-aging-services) → procedure/therapy demand
    (medtech-devices, biotech-genomics) ; glp1-obesity-metabolic cross-cuts all three
    (CGM tailwind for devices, capital spillover into biotech, cost-trend swing for managed care).
  • Financial rails: fintech-payments (card networks, A2A) ⟷ tokenization-digital-assets
    (stablecoins/RWA threaten to disintermediate card rails) ; UPI/PromptPay tie to
    india-em-growth / asean-thailand.
  • Geographic rotation: us-megacap-quality (concentration) ⟷ india-em-growth /
    japan-corporate-reform / china-contrarian / asean-thailand (the rotation away from US).

The diversification-killers. Themes in the same cluster rise and fall together regardless of

4. Key disruption / tension edges

Where one theme is a threat to another (not just a feeder):
- tokenization-digital-assets (stablecoins/A2A) → disrupts → fintech-payments (card rails).
- glp1-obesity-metabolic → debated headwind → medtech-devices (bariatric/some cardiac/ortho)
but tailwind for CGM — net effect unresolved.
- ai-software-agents → disrupts → legacy per-seat SaaS (within its own theme) and parts of
fintech-payments (agentic commerce).
- renewables-storage + nuclear-uranium → compete for the same power-grid-electrification
baseload demand (and policy/capital).
- quantum-computing → long-tail threat → cybersecurity (post-quantum cryptography) —
also a demand driver for it.
- china-contrarian (BYD, China AI) → competes with → critical-minerals supply security,
semiconductors (export controls), us-megacap-quality AI leadership.

5. Practical implications

  1. "Diversified across themes" can still be one bet. Holding ai-infrastructure + semis + power
    + gas + cooling is ~5 expressions of the same AI-capex thesis (cluster 1).
  2. Real diversification comes from combining cluster 1 with the genuine diversifiers (defense,
    gold, healthcare/aging, GLP-1) and from the rate/USD-independent themes.
  3. The macro switch (macro-regime) sits above this map: rates/USD/liquidity decide which
    clusters lead. A rate shock hits cluster 2; a risk-off hits cluster 5; a USD spike hits cluster 4.
