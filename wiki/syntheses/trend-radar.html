Trend Radar

Bottom line: The master dashboard of long-term investment megatrends — now structured into
7 meta-forces → ~28 themes, each with conviction, maturity stage, horizon, and the cleanest
instruments. Rewritten every scan (CLAUDE.md §10). Cross-theme dependencies live in
theme-linkage-map; the macro overlay conditioning all of them in macro-regime.
Insight only — not buy/sell advice.

Legend. Conviction 1–5 (5 = highest risk-adjusted — nothing earns a 5). Stage:
emerging → accelerating → mature → fading. Horizon = years to thesis playing out. Instruments
are candidates to research, not recommendations. Figures sourced on each theme page,
tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference).

Scan notes — 2026-06-01 (analyst-grade rebuild)

  • The dominant structure remains one correlated complex: AI → power → semiconductors → cooling
    → gas.     Most conviction-4 themes in meta-forces 1–2 draw on the same AI-capex demand engine.
    That concentration is the radar's single biggest correlated-de-rating risk — a single AI-capex
    air-pocket re-rates them together. See the shared-driver clusters in theme-linkage-map.
  • Deliberate diversifiers (low AI-capex correlation): defense-security,
    infrastructure-capex (partly), commodities-gold-real-assets, healthcare-aging-services,
    japan-corporate-reform. These are why the radar isn't a one-way AI bet.
  • Nothing earns a 5. The highest-conviction themes are also the richest-valued. High conviction
    in the trend ≠ attractive entry today. Convictions are risk-adjusted, not enthusiasm.
  • Geographic rotation is live: 2025 saw ex-US (MSCI World ex-US +31.8%) beat the S&P (+17.7%)
    with a −9.5% USD — the first real crack in US exceptionalism. india-em-growth and
    japan-corporate-reform are the structural beneficiaries; us-megacap-quality held at 3 on
    concentration/valuation.
  • Speculative tail kept honest: space-economy (2.5) and quantum-computing (2) are
    small, binary, momentum-priced sleeves — included for completeness, sized accordingly.
  • Macro overlay: rates/USD/liquidity condition every theme — see macro-regime. Rate-sensitive
    themes (water utilities, senior-housing REITs, gold, EM) move together on the rate axis.
