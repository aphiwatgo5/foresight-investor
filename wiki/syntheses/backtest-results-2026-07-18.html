The regime split (the most decision-relevant view)

Momentum is the highest-return sleeve but deeply regime-dependent — it makes ~+100%/yr
annualized in bull days and loses ~−46%/yr in bear days. This is exactly the regime-sensitivity
the macro-rotation sleeve exists to manage.

Implication: a portfolio that holds raw momentum through a bear regime gives back 2 years of
alpha. The macro-rotation sleeve (which tilts defensive in bear regimes via low-vol + low-DD
selection) is the structural fix. The ensemble's 25% momentum weight is itself a dampener —
full-tilt momentum would have done +29% CAGR but with a -40% drawdown; the ensemble gives up
~10pts of CAGR for a 10pt drawdown improvement.

Caveats + honest limitations

  1. Value/quality sleeves use point-in-time fundamentals — the historical P/E + margins come
    from the current snapshot only, not a true point-in-time history. This biases the value sleeve
    toward names that are currently cheap     (which may have been expensive historically). Fix:
    wire SEC EDGAR historicals; flagged as the #1 v4.0 enhancement.
  2. 5y window is one regime (mostly bull) — the period 2021-2026 had 799 bull / 238 bear / 219
    sideways days. The bear-regime metrics come from a thin sample. The March-2020 COVID crash is
    not in the window — extend to 7y to capture it.
  3. No transaction costs — the sleeves rebalance monthly at ~30-50% turnover; real costs
    (spread + commission + tax) would reduce alpha by ~1-2%/yr.
  4. Top-20 equal-weight is arbitrary; cap-weighting or conviction-weighting may do better.
  5. Survivorship: the universe is the current 272 names — names that went bankrupt/delisted
    over the 5y aren't in the sample (mild survivorship bias).

These caveats are standard for a v1 quant backtest and do not change the qualitative read
(all sleeves beat VOO with reasonable risk-adjustment). They are the v4.0 enhancement backlog.
