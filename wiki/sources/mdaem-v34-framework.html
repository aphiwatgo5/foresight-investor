Key takeaways

  • The framework is a gated, weighted, scenario-driven decision pipeline for single names — see mdaem-framework for the full methodology.
  • It calibrates against Seeking Alpha Quant ratings (seeking-alpha-quant) and takes the lower of its own vs SA's signal when they conflict.
  • Its distinguishing feature is the validation loop: dated, falsifiable predictions reviewed every 3 months, with misses codified as numbered refinements (21 so far). Captured in mdaem-validation-database.
  • Mandate and capital are concrete: ~$83.2K US book + ~$40K China/HK book on the Dime! app (Thai retail investor). MAX RETURN, asymmetric preference.
  • Critical distinction: MDAEM does position sizing and explicit ADD/REJECT verdicts — the opposite of this vault's insight-only guardrail. Documented here as Aphiwat's own decision tool, not adopted as wiki behavior. See mdaem-vs-futurist-investor.

Summary

MDAEM v3.4 reached maturity through "a catastrophic v1 failure" and successive iterations. The core engine: a 60-second triage, then 10 hard gates (any fail → reject), then 10 weighted dimensions scored 1–10 to a /100 total mapping to tiers (T1 85+ down to REJECT <40). A Seeking Alpha Quant overlay caps sizing. Scenario math (explicit bull/base/bear probabilities → expected value + asymmetry ratio) is mandatory before sizing. Every decision is logged with measurable 3-month success/failure criteria.

The framework's "nervous system" is the validation database: it treats every call as a falsifiable prediction and reviews outcomes quarterly, converting patterns-in-misses into new refinements (A–O plus 16–21). Refinements 16–21 came from real 2026 cases — DDD reflexive bias, KALV M&A ceiling, ONDS/TNGX late-cycle, QBTS/RGTI extreme valuation, VKTX pre-catalyst dilution.

Its stated ethos: "You cannot have selective discipline" and "Discipline matters more than insight."
