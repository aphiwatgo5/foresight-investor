Cybersecurity

Definition: Software, services, and platforms protecting enterprise IT/cloud/identity/
endpoints — endpoint/XDR, network/firewall + SASE/zero-trust, identity & access, cloud security
(CSPM/CNAPP/CASB), and consolidating security platforms. Conviction 4 on the trend-radar. Its
key attraction: near-non-discretionary demand that is structurally less AI-capex-cyclical than
the rest of the Compute & AI stack — a diversifier from the shared AI engine
(see theme-linkage-map).

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

Pillars: endpoint/XDR (CrowdStrike, SentinelOne), network/firewall + SASE/zero-trust (Palo Alto,
Fortinet, Zscaler, Cloudflare), identity & privileged access (Okta, CyberArk), cloud security —
CSPM/CNAPP/CASB (Palo Alto Prisma, Microsoft/Wiz, Zscaler), and consolidating platforms.
Increasingly intersects with ai-software-agents via AI-driven detection and the new "AI-agent
identity" attack surface.

3. Key structural drivers

Demand side: rising threat volume/sophistication; AI as both attacker and defender (Gartner
names GenAI — internal and adversarial — as a key growth driver); regulatory/compliance; cloud
migration creating new attack surfaces (drives CSPM/CNAPP); zero-trust mandates; cyber-insurance
requirements. Supply side: platform consolidation ("single-vendor SASE," vendor
platformization) raising switching costs; AI-native detection lowering analyst cost; identity
becoming the new perimeter. Spend is non-discretionary / counter-cyclical vs AI-software
capex — the key differentiator.

4. Value-chain / linkage

A horizontal tax on all digital activity, including the AI buildout: more cloud + more AI
agents = more attack surface = more security spend. Direct linkage to ai-software-agents: AI
agents need identities and privileged-access governance (CyberArk thesis); AI accelerates both
attacks and defenses. Linkage to ai-infrastructure: securing AI data-center/cloud workloads.
Less semiconductor-dependent than the rest of the stack — demand is threat/compliance-driven, not
GPU-capex-driven — which is why it offers diversification from the AI-cyclical engine.

5. Competitive landscape — consolidation is the story

Structurally consolidating around platforms. Palo Alto's $25B acquisition of CyberArk
(announced 2025-07-30, closed 2026-02-11; $45 cash + 2.2005 PANW shares per CyberArk share,
~26% premium, ~17.8x fwd revenue) added an identity pillar — one of the largest cyber deals
ever (fact). "Platformization" — customers consolidating point products onto fewer vendors (Palo
Alto, CrowdStrike, Microsoft, Fortinet). Moats/margins: Fortinet highest margin (owns ASIC
hardware); CrowdStrike/Zscaler/Cloudflare high-growth SaaS at premium multiples; identity (Okta,
now CyberArk-in-PANW) the contested high-value layer. AI-native detection is the new competitive
axis (Zscaler AI-security ARR +>80% YoY, beat its $400M FY26 target three quarters early — fact).

7. Competitiveness read

Winning: Palo Alto (platformization + CyberArk identity pillar = broadest stack),
CrowdStrike (endpoint platform leader, record profitability, but ~20x sales), and Zscaler
(zero-trust pure-play with explosive AI-security ARR, record 23% margins). These three drove a 2026
cyber rally that outran chips — but one analysis frames it as a supply-consolidation rally, not
a pure demand story (record M&A multiples), warranting caution. Pressured: Okta and
standalone identity players now face a much larger integrated PANW-CyberArk competitor; Check Point
and legacy network vendors are the value/low-growth cohort; SentinelOne is the cheap #2 endpoint
optionality play (~4.6x sales) but must prove it can close the gap with CrowdStrike. The demand
backdrop is the sector's best feature: near-non-discretionary spend growing low-teens regardless of
macro.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Valuation extremes: Cloudflare ~27.6x EV/Sales and CrowdStrike ~20.6x are priced for
    perfection — any growth slip or self-inflicted breach/outage (CrowdStrike-2024-style) de-rates
    sharply.
  • Consolidation = rally without demand: if the 2026 surge is M&A-multiple-driven (CyberArk deal
    repricing peers) rather than spend-driven, multiples could mean-revert.
  • Platform consolidation cuts both ways: winners (PANW, CRWD) take share; point-product names
    (Okta, SentinelOne, Check Point) get squeezed.
  • AI disruption: could commoditize some detection layers; also raises attacker capability faster
    than defense (net demand-positive, but volatile).
  • FX / Israel-exposure (CHKP, ex-CyberArk) and government-budget sensitivity.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 4/5 (calibrated — strong, near-non-discretionary demand and diversification from
    the AI-capex cycle support it; rich leader multiples and "consolidation-rally" caution prevent a
    5).
  • Stage: accelerating (12.5–13% spend growth; cloud-security sub-segments 26–31% CAGR).
  • Horizon: 3–7+ years (secular, threat-driven demand is durable).
  • Invalidation: info-security spend growth falls below ~8% YoY (Gartner), OR leader multiples
    (CRWD/NET) compress and ARR growth decelerates below ~15% simultaneously, OR a major
    platform-vendor self-inflicted breach breaks the platformization trust premise.
