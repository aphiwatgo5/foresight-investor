Fintech & Payments

Definition: The end-to-end electronic movement of value — card networks (Visa, Mastercard),
processors/acquirers (Fiserv, Global Payments, Adyen, Stripe-private), digital wallets & P2P (PayPal,
Block), BNPL (Affirm), and digitally-native banks/neobanks (Nubank) — with an AI-in-fintech overlay
(fraud, underwriting, agentic commerce). A barbell: a fortress duopoly at the center, a contested
periphery. Conviction 3 at the theme level on the trend-radar (networks alone merit 4).

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

(a) Card networks — Visa, Mastercard (toll-road economics, ~50–65% op margins); (b) processors/
acquirers — Fiserv, Global Payments, Adyen, Stripe (private); (c) wallets/P2P — PayPal, Block
(Cash App); (d) BNPL — Affirm; (e) neobanks — Nubank. AI-in-fintech is the cross-cutting driver
that most benefits the largest proprietary-data holders.

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: secular cash-to-digital shift (esp. EM — Latam/SE Asia); e-commerce + cross-border travel
(the highest-margin network volume); embedded finance / account-to-account; younger cohorts adopting
BNPL + neobanks (Nubank >135M customers). Supply: real-time rails (FedNow, Pix, UPI) lowering
friction; AI underwriting/fraud cutting losses and expanding credit; value-added services (data,
security, loyalty) becoming the margin engine; stablecoin settlement emerging as a new low-cost rail
(tokenization-digital-assets).

4. Value-chain / linkage

Consumer/merchant → acquirer/processor (Fiserv, GPN, Adyen, Stripe) → network rails (Visa, Mastercard)
→ issuer bank → interchange flows back. Wallets (PayPal, Cash App) + BNPL (Affirm) sit at the front-end,
often riding on top of the networks they nominally disrupt. Neobanks (Nubank) verticalize the whole
stack in one geography. Stablecoins (tokenization-digital-assets) threaten to disintermediate parts
of the chain over a multi-year horizon. Cross-border volumes tie to macro-regime (travel, FX).

5. Competitive landscape

A barbell. At the center, Visa + Mastercard form a near-duopoly with extreme moats — global
two-sided network effects, regulatory entrenchment, 50%+ op margins — among the highest-quality
compounders in the market. The periphery (PayPal, Block, Adyen, Affirm, neobanks) is far more contested,
lower-margin, and exposed to competition, credit cycles, and disruption. Processors (Fiserv, GPN) are
scale-driven, mature, and decelerating. Moats range from durable (network effects, licenses) to fragile
(brand, UX).

7. Competitiveness read

Investable quality is heavily concentrated in Visa and Mastercard: double-digit revenue growth on
~50–65% op margins, fortress balance sheets, and a structural toll on global commerce — compounders, not
turnarounds; their main risk is regulatory/disruption, not competition. Adyen and Nubank are the
highest-quality peripheral growers — Adyen for single-platform enterprise tech (53% EBITDA margin),
Nubank for category-leading neobank economics (29% ROE) and a long Latam/US runway (with EM-macro/credit
risk). The contested tier — PayPal, Block, Fiserv, GPN — shows the strain of maturity: PayPal grew
only 7% with compressing margins; Fiserv revenue shrank 2%. Affirm is a high-beta growth story (GMV
+36%) turning profitable but structurally exposed to credit/rates. AI-in-fintech is the swing factor and
most benefits incumbents with the largest data sets.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Regulation — interchange caps, antitrust on the V/MA duopoly, BNPL/credit rules, neobank capital reqs.
  • Disintermediation — A2A rails (Pix, UPI, FedNow) and stablecoin settlement bypassing card rails.
  • Credit cycle — BNPL (Affirm) and neobank (Nubank) loan books exposed to defaults/EM macro.
  • Margin compression / maturity — PayPal, Fiserv, GPN decelerating or shrinking.
  • FX & EM macro — Nubank, cross-border volumes, Adyen euro reporting.
  • Big Tech & agentic-AI commerce reshaping front-end economics.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 3/5 (theme overall) — Visa/Mastercard individually merit a 4 (rare moat + double-digit
    growth + high margins), but the contested, decelerating periphery and disintermediation risk pull the
    blended theme to 3.
  • Stage: mature-but-compounding (networks) / contested (periphery). Horizon: 3–7 years.
  • Invalidation: material adverse interchange/antitrust ruling on V/MA, OR sustained stablecoin/A2A
    volume migration off card rails, OR a BNPL/neobank credit blowup.
