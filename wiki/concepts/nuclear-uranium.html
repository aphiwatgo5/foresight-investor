Nuclear & Uranium

Definition: The nuclear fuel cycle and reactor build-out — uranium miners, enrichment/
conversion, utilities with nuclear fleets, and the emerging small modular reactor (SMR) vendors.
The "clean baseload" answer to AI data-center power demand (ai-infrastructure,
power-grid-electrification). A meaningful but longer-dated, execution-heavy sub-theme of
energy-transition. Conviction 3 on the trend-radar — real demand inflection, but timelines
and SMR commercialization remain speculative.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

Two distinct layers: (a) the fuel cycle — uranium mining (Cameco, Kazatomprom), conversion,
enrichment (Centrus), and physical-uranium holders (Sprott SPUT); and (b) the reactor layer —
utilities operating existing fleets (Constellation, Vistra) and SMR developers (NuScale, Oklo,
X-energy). Links to power-grid-electrification (the grid that nuclear feeds),
ai-infrastructure (hyperscaler PPAs/restarts), critical-minerals (uranium as a strategic
mineral), and natural-gas-lng (the competing dispatchable baseload).

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: AI/data-center baseload appetite (hyperscaler nuclear PPAs — Microsoft–Constellation
Three Mile Island restart, Amazon–Talen/Vistra, Google–Kairos); government pro-nuclear policy
(COP28 "triple nuclear by 2050" pledge, US ADVANCE Act, restarts in Japan); electrification.
Supply (bullish for uranium price): Kazatomprom/Cameco production discipline and misses; thin
secondary supply; Russian enrichment dependence pushing Western enrichment build-out (Centrus).
Headwinds: spot pulled back from the Jan-2026 ~$101 peak to ~$85; SMR commercialization is
years out and capital-intensive.

4. Value-chain / linkage

Uranium mining (CCJ, KAP) → conversion → enrichment (LEU/Centrus, Urenco) → fuel fabrication →
reactors (utility fleets CEG/VST; SMR vendors OKLO/SMR/X-energy) → baseload power →
power-grid-electrification → ai-infrastructure (data-center load). Competes with
natural-gas-lng as dispatchable baseload; complements renewables-storage (24/7 vs
intermittent). Uranium itself is a critical-minerals strategic input.

5. Competitive landscape

Uranium mining is a concentrated oligopoly — Kazatomprom (largest, lowest-cost ISR) and Cameco
(largest Western, tier-1 Athabasca assets: McArthur River, Cigar Lake) dominate, giving real
pricing leverage in a deficit. Enrichment is even more concentrated (Urenco, Orano, Rosatom,
Centrus) with high Western-supply-security value post-Russia. SMR vendors are pre-revenue /
early-revenue with binary outcomes — first-mover design wins and regulatory approval (NRC) are the
moats, but commercialization risk is high. Utilities with existing nuclear fleets (CEG, VST)
hold the scarcest asset: operating 24/7 clean baseload that hyperscalers will pay premiums for.

7. Competitiveness read

Winning / highest quality: the utilities with existing fleets — Constellation (largest US
nuclear, TMI restart, Microsoft PPA) and Vistra (AWS PPA) — monetize scarce 24/7 clean baseload
today, with the least technology risk; they overlap with power-grid-electrification. Among
miners, Cameco (Western tier-1 grades + Westinghouse optionality) and Kazatomprom (low-cost
swing producer) have the cleanest leverage to a structurally tight uranium market. Centrus
(LEU) is a focused way to play Western enrichment security / HALEU scarcity.

Speculative / watch: SMR vendors (Oklo, NuScale) are the highest-beta, most narrative-driven
names — large addressable prize but pre-/minimal-revenue, capital-hungry, and dependent on
multi-year NRC licensing and first-of-a-kind construction. They can re-rate violently in both
directions. Uranium equities also pulled back with spot from the ~$101 Jan-2026 peak.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Spot price reversal: uranium already fell from ~$101 (Jan'26) to ~$85 (May'26); a demand
    air-pocket or Kazatomprom supply surge compresses miner margins.
  • SMR disappointment: licensing delays, cost overruns, or a high-profile project cancellation
    (NuScale's UAMPS termination precedent) de-rates the whole SMR cohort.
  • AI-capex correlation: the data-center-PPA demand leg is correlated with the broader
    ai-infrastructure complex (see theme-linkage-map).
  • Accident / sentiment risk: any nuclear safety event re-freezes policy and public support.
  • Long timelines: new builds and SMRs are 5–10+ year stories — capital is tied up with
    execution risk before payoff.

9. Investable instruments (candidates — not recommendations)

  • URA (Global X Uranium) — miners + nuclear-fuel-cycle equities; largest uranium ETF.
  • URNM (Sprott Uranium Miners) — purer miner/physical tilt.
  • NLR (VanEck Uranium & Nuclear) — broader utility/reactor tilt.
  • SPUT (Sprott Physical Uranium Trust) — direct physical-uranium exposure (spot proxy).
  • Representative securities: CCJ, KAP.L, CEG, VST, LEU, OKLO, SMR, UEC, DNN.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 3/5 — genuine structural demand inflection (AI baseload + policy + restarts) and a
    supply-constrained uranium market, but held below the AI-equipment themes by (a) SMR
    commercialization risk and long timelines, (b) uranium-spot volatility, and (c) the cleanest
    "AI power" expression arguably being grid/equipment (power-grid-electrification) rather than
    the fuel cycle.
  • Stage: accelerating (utilities/fuel) to emerging (SMRs). Horizon: 3–10 years.
  • Invalidation: uranium spot breaks back below ~$70/lb on supply normalization, OR a flagship
    SMR program is cancelled/slips multi-year, OR hyperscaler nuclear PPAs stall.
