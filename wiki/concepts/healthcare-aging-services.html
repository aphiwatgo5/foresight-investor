Healthcare & Aging / Services

Definition: Healthcare delivery and financing rather than drug/device innovation — managed care/
insurers (UnitedHealth, Elevance), hospitals (HCA), distribution (McKesson), and healthcare real
estate / senior housing REITs (Welltower). The unifying driver is demographic aging: a rising 65+/80+
population consuming disproportionate healthcare and housing resources. Conviction 3 on the
trend-radar — the tailwind is durable, but the vehicles carry heavy policy/rate risk.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

(a) Managed care — UnitedHealth (+Optum), Elevance, CVS/Aetna; (b) hospitals — HCA; (c)
distribution — McKesson, Cencora, Cardinal (three-firm oligopoly); (d) healthcare REITs / senior
housing — Welltower. The senior-housing sub-theme is the cleanest secular winner; managed care is a
recovering-but-volatile turnaround.

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: the 80+ cohort (+~36.6% this decade) drives utilization + senior-housing need; per-capita
senior spend ~5x a child's mechanically raises system spend. Supply (favorable for housing):
senior-housing construction starts at multi-decade lows create a supply-demand imbalance and pricing
power (Welltower NOI margins >25%, 14+ quarters of 20%+ same-store NOI growth). Policy headwind
(managed care): CMS Medicare-Advantage funding cuts + IRA pressured insurer margins in 2025; medical
cost-trend is the swing variable.

4. Value-chain / linkage

Payers/insurers (UNH, ELV) collect premiums + manage medical cost (MCR is the margin lever) → providers/
hospitals (HCA) deliver care → distributors (McKesson) move drugs → REITs (Welltower) own the physical
real estate. UnitedHealth's Optum uniquely spans payer + provider + pharmacy. Aging demographics raise
volume across the whole chain; links to glp1-obesity-metabolic (cost-trend + distribution volume)
and medtech-devices/biotech-genomics (procedure + therapy demand). REIT valuations track
macro-regime rates.

5. Competitive landscape

Scaled oligopolies per layer: managed care dominated by UNH + Elevance (high regulatory/scale barriers);
hospitals are regional-density games (HCA); distribution is a three-firm oligopoly (thin margins, huge
scale moats); senior-housing REITs led by Welltower (cost-of-capital + operator relationships). Moats:
scale, regulatory complexity, vertical integration (Optum), and — for REITs — irreplaceable real estate
plus a financing-cost advantage.

7. Competitiveness read

The 2025–26 managed-care story is a sharp reversal. UnitedHealth had a genuine 2025 crisis — FY25
MCR blew out to 88.9% (from 85.5%) on CMS cuts/IRA/cost trend, compressing EBIT margin toward ~3%. But
Q1'26 marked recovery: MCR improved to 83.9%, adjusted EPS $7.23, and the stock jumped ~37% in April
2026 on the beat + guidance raise. Near-term UNH is winning back confidence, though the margin recovery
is fragile and utilization remains elevated. Elevance is steady (+2.9%, modest beat). The clearest
structural winner, however, is Welltower: senior housing has a near-ideal setup — record demand (19
straight quarters of absorption), multi-decade-low new supply, NOI margins >25%, and 14+ quarters of
20%+ same-store NOI growth, all backed by the unstoppable 80+ wave. Hospitals (HCA) and distributors
(McKesson) are stable scale-compounders but lower-growth. Net: Welltower is the cleanest secular winner;
managed care is a recovering-but-volatile turnaround; distribution/hospitals are defensive ballast.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Managed-care policy risk — further CMS/Medicare-Advantage rate cuts, IRA effects, political
    scrutiny of insurer margins.
  • Medical-cost-trend volatility — elevated utilization can re-break the MCR any quarter.
  • REIT interest-rate sensitivity — Welltower's valuation/cost of capital hinge on rates (macro-regime).
  • Hospital labor costs and reimbursement pressure.
  • Distributor margins structurally thin; opioid-litigation tails; drug-pricing reform.
  • Senior-housing operator/staffing execution risk despite favorable supply.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 3/5 (theme overall) — the aging tailwind is about as durable as any theme exists, but
    the investable vehicles carry heavy policy/regulatory risk (managed care) or rate sensitivity (REITs)
    that cap risk-adjusted conviction. The senior-housing sub-theme (Welltower) individually merits a 4;
    managed care is a 3 (recovering but policy-whipsawed).
  • Stage: mature with an accelerating senior-housing sub-segment. Horizon: 5–15 years (demographic).
  • Invalidation: a structural Medicare-Advantage reimbursement reset that permanently impairs insurer
    economics, OR a sustained high-rate regime that breaks REIT spreads.
