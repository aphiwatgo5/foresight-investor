AI Infrastructure

Definition: The physical and silicon build-out enabling large-scale AI training and
inference — hyperscale data centers, accelerator compute (GPUs/custom ASICs), the servers/racks
that house them, and the capital and power behind them. The flagship megatrend on the
trend-radar (conviction 4, accelerating) and the demand engine that pulls
semiconductors, networking-optical, and energy-transition. Interconnect is split out
to networking-optical; power to energy-transition.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

Spans the demand source (hyperscaler capex) down through compute silicon (NVIDIA GPUs, AMD
Instinct, Broadcom/Marvell custom ASICs), systems integration (Super Micro, Dell, server ODMs),
and the facility layer (Vertiv power/cooling, data-center REITs). Excludes the interconnect
fabric (networking-optical) and the power/grid constraint (energy-transition), which have
their own pages.

4. Value-chain / linkage

Upstream foundry (TSMC) + HBM memory + EDA → compute silicon (NVIDIA, AMD, Broadcom/Marvell
ASICs) → systems & integration (Super Micro, Dell, ODMs) → facility layer (Vertiv
power/cooling, REITs Equinix/Digital Realty) → hyperscalers/neoclouds (MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN,
META, Oracle, CoreWeave) → AI software/apps (ai-software-agents, the monetization layer
that must eventually justify the capex). Adjacent: semiconductors (foundry/HBM),
energy-transition (the binding power constraint), networking-optical (interconnect),
cybersecurity (securing AI/cloud workloads).

5. Competitive landscape

  • Accelerator compute: highly concentrated. NVIDIA dominant (~$5.11T mkt cap, 1 Jun 2026),
    71% gross margins; AMD a credible #2 (DC $5.8B/qtr, +57% YoY) but far smaller; custom ASICs
    (Broadcom, Marvell) capturing hyperscaler in-house silicon spend.
  • Moats sit in: CUDA software ecosystem + networking (NVIDIA); custom-ASIC design + IP +
    hyperscaler relationships (Broadcom, Marvell); power/thermal know-how + installed base (Vertiv).
  • Margin map: silicon designers earn the fattest margins (NVDA ~71% GM, AVGO ~78% GM);
    integrators (Super Micro, Dell) thin-margin/high-volume; facilities (Vertiv) mid-margin but high
    backlog visibility.

7. Competitiveness read

Winning: NVIDIA is the structural winner — FY2026 DC revenue $193.7B (+68%) at 71% gross
margin is an extraordinary scale-plus-pricing-power combination anchored by CUDA lock-in and the
bundling of compute + networking. Broadcom is the clearest secondary winner: as hyperscalers
internalize silicon, AVGO captures custom-ASIC design spend at ~78% GM without competing head-on
with NVIDIA. Vertiv wins the "picks-and-shovels" facility layer — power/cooling is the physical
bottleneck (orders +252% YoY). AMD is winning share (DC +57%) but from a small base and at lower
margin per dollar.

Pressured: Pure integrators (Super Micro, Dell) carry the capex volume but capture thin
margins and bear inventory/component-cost risk. Hyperscalers are simultaneously the demand engine
and the entities absorbing capital risk — FCF compresses (Oracle ~ -$10B FCF est.) as they front
the buildout ahead of monetization.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Circular financing: analysts identify >$800B of interlinked arrangements (NVIDIA→OpenAI
    ~$100B; NVIDIA stake in CoreWeave; Oracle/Stargate→OpenAI; MSFT ~27% of OpenAI) where chip/cloud
    vendors fund customers who buy back their own products (est., 2026). A confidence break cascades.
  • AI-revenue gap / capex digestion: OpenAI reportedly on track to lose ~$14B in 2026 (est.).
    If end-demand monetization lags the ~$630–725B 2026 capex, a digestion air-pocket de-rates the
    whole chain. First cracks already shown (Alphabet capex-guide sell-off; AI-infra selloff
    2026-04-28).
  • Correlation risk: five of six conviction-4 radar trends share this one demand engine — a
    single AI-capex disappointment de-rates them together (see theme-linkage-map).
  • Power constraint: grid/power availability is the gating factor (energy-transition).
  • Single-name dependence: the thesis leans heavily on NVIDIA execution + continued hyperscaler
    willingness to spend.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 4/5 (calibrated — not a 5: richest-valued cohort with circular-financing and
    digestion overhangs).
  • Stage: accelerating (capex still inflecting +60–77% YoY into 2026).

  • Invalidation: a hyperscaler cutting 2027 capex guidance YoY, OR a public AI-monetization
    shortfall forcing order cancellations, OR NVIDIA DC revenue declining sequentially 2+ quarters.
