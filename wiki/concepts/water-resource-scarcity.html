Water & Resource Scarcity

Definition: The investable response to freshwater scarcity — regulated water utilities
(American Water), water-technology and flow-control (Xylem, Veralto, Pentair), and water-treatment
chemicals (Ecolab) — now with an accelerating data-center cooling-water sub-theme as AI
compute drives explosive water demand. A defensive, demand-inelastic theme with a new growth
overlay. Conviction 4 on the trend-radar.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

(a) Regulated utilities — American Water Works (AWK — rate-base compounder); (b) water tech/

Veralto (VLTO), Ecolab (ECL — water/hygiene chemicals). Cross-cutting driver: scarcity + quality
regulation + the new AI-datacenter water-demand spike (links to data-center-cooling).

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: population growth + urbanization; aging water infrastructure needing trillions in
replacement (US esp.); tightening water-quality regulation (PFAS, lead-pipe replacement); and a
new, fast-growing data-center cooling-water demand wave (~11.84% CAGR) as AI compute scales
(data-center-cooling, ai-infrastructure). Supply: scarcity itself — climate stress,
droughts, depleting aquifers — makes water rights, treatment tech, and efficiency increasingly
valuable. Regulated utilities earn allowed returns on rising rate base.

4. Value-chain / linkage

Source/utilities (AWK own water rights + distribution, earn regulated returns) → equipment/flow
(Xylem, Pentair pumps/metering) → treatment/analytics (Veralto, Ecolab chemicals + monitoring) →
end users (municipal, industrial, data centers). The AI-datacenter cooling-water demand is
the key new linkage to data-center-cooling / ai-infrastructure / power-grid-electrification.
Utility valuations are rate-sensitive (macro-regime). Adjacent to infrastructure-capex
(water infrastructure spend).

5. Competitive landscape

Defensive, moat-rich structure: regulated utilities (AWK) are local monopolies with rate-base
compounding and acquisition roll-up of fragmented municipal systems; water-tech (Xylem, Pentair)
has installed-base + specification moats; Ecolab/Veralto have switching-cost-heavy chemical/
service models. Moats: regulatory monopoly + rate base (utilities), installed base + service
contracts (tech/chemicals), and scale. Demand is highly inelastic — water is non-discretionary.

7. Competitiveness read

Water is a defensive compounder with a new growth kicker. The core — regulated utilities (AWK)
and water-tech/treatment (Xylem, Veralto, Ecolab) — offers inelastic demand, monopoly/installed-
base moats, and steady rate-base or service-driven compounding: classic all-weather quality. What
upgrades the theme to a 4 is the AI-datacenter cooling-water demand wave (~2.97T liters in
2026, ~11.84% CAGR) — a genuine, fast-growing new demand source layered onto an already-scarce
resource, directly linked to the data-center-cooling and ai-infrastructure build-out.
American Water is the purest regulated compounder; Xylem and Veralto are the best ways to play
the tech/efficiency + datacenter angle. The main caution is that utilities are rate-sensitive
(valuations compress in high-rate regimes) and the tech names are not cheap. Net: high-quality,
durable, with rare defensive + AI-linked-growth characteristics — one of the cleaner cross-cutting
themes.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Interest-rate sensitivity — regulated-utility valuations compress when rates rise (macro-regime).
  • Regulatory/rate-case risk — allowed returns can be cut by regulators.
  • Valuation — quality water names trade at premium multiples.
  • DC water-demand backlash — local opposition / regulation could curb data-center water use
    (which would also accelerate data-center-cooling closed-loop/liquid solutions).
  • Capex/execution on infrastructure replacement; weather/drought variability.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 4/5 — a rare combination of defensive, inelastic-demand quality (utilities/
    treatment) plus a structural new-growth overlay (AI-datacenter cooling water). Durable moats
    and an unstoppable scarcity backdrop. Held below 5 by rate sensitivity and premium valuations.
  • Stage: mature core + accelerating data-center sub-theme. Horizon: 5–10 years.
  • Invalidation: a sustained high-rate regime that structurally de-rates utilities, OR
    regulatory rate-case reversals that impair the rate-base compounding model.
