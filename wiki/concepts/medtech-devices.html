MedTech & Devices

Definition: Medical devices and equipment spanning surgical robotics (Intuitive Surgical),
cardiovascular/structural heart and electrophysiology (Boston Scientific, Abbott, Medtronic, Edwards),
orthopedics/neuro (Stryker), and continuous glucose monitoring (DexCom, Abbott). Durable,
recurring-revenue franchises with razor-and-blade economics and high switching costs. Conviction 4 on
the trend-radar.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

(a) Surgical robotics — Intuitive (soft tissue, ~60%+ share, ~85% recurring); (b) structural
heart / EP / cardiology — Boston Scientific, Abbott, Medtronic, Edwards (TAVR); (c) ortho/neuro —
Stryker (Mako); (d) CGM — DexCom, Abbott (Libre), Medtronic. The razor-and-blade model (place a
system, sell single-use instruments forever) is the economic core.

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: aging demographics → procedure volumes (cardiac, ortho, neuro); diabetes/metabolic
prevalence + GLP-1-driven metabolic awareness expands CGM TAM (a positive glp1-obesity-metabolic
spillover); structural heart (TAVR/LAAC) replacing open surgery. Supply/tech: robotics adoption
compounds via installed base + recurring instruments (~85% recurring at Intuitive); AI integration into
imaging/robotics; switching costs from trained-surgeon ecosystems + consumables lock-in.

4. Value-chain / linkage

Device OEMs design capital equipment (robots, ablation/imaging) + recurring consumables → hospitals/ASCs
buy → physicians (trained on specific platforms) influence. GLP-1 is the key cross-link: tailwind for
CGM/metabolic monitoring, debated headwind for bariatric and some cardiac/ortho volumes
(glp1-obesity-metabolic). Demand tracks healthcare-aging-services demographics.

5. Competitive landscape

Scaled incumbents compete within franchise verticals rather than head-to-head: Intuitive owns
soft-tissue robotics (~60%+, ~85% recurring), Boston Scientific + Abbott lead structural heart/EP,
Stryker leads ortho/neuro, Medtronic is broad/diversified, DexCom/Abbott split CGM. Moats: installed
base + consumables lock-in, surgeon-training ecosystems, clinical evidence/regulatory approvals, scale
in R&D + salesforce.

7. Competitiveness read

Intuitive Surgical is the standout winner — +23% revenue, a >20% EPS beat, dominant share and ~85%
recurring revenue make it the highest-quality compounder in the group. Boston Scientific is the
clear secondary winner (~12% reported / ~9% organic, stock up post-print) riding structural heart and
EP. The relative loser this quarter is Stryker, which missed both revenue and adjusted EPS — a
notable stumble, though one quarter doesn't break the franchise. Medtronic is the steady,
slower-growing incumbent (~4.8% organic) — defensive, not exciting. In CGM, Abbott holds the volume
crown (~57%, Libre scale) while DexCom is the faster-growing focused player (+15%); both benefit if
GLP-1 expands metabolic monitoring. Net: quality and growth concentrate in ISRG and BSX; SYK is the
show-me name near-term.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • GLP-1 procedure headwind — weight-loss drugs could reduce bariatric and possibly some cardiac/
    ortho volumes over time.
  • Hospital capital-spending cycles — robotics/capital equipment sales are budget-sensitive.
  • Pricing/reimbursement pressure and hospital cost containment.
  • Robotics competition (Medtronic Hugo, J&J Ottava) eroding Intuitive's moat over time.
  • CGM commoditization as Abbott-DexCom compete and OTC/pharmacy channels expand.
  • Single-quarter execution risk (Stryker miss) and FX exposure.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 4/5 — durable, recurring-revenue franchises with strong moats and a clean demographic
    tailwind; ISRG and BSX are best-in-class compounders. Held below 5 by the GLP-1 procedure-demand
    overhang, capex cyclicality, and intensifying robotics competition.
  • Stage: accelerating (robotics/CGM) to mature (diversified). Horizon: 5–10 years.
  • Invalidation: clear evidence GLP-1 drugs structurally shrink high-margin procedure volumes, OR
    Intuitive losing share to Medtronic/J&J robotics.
