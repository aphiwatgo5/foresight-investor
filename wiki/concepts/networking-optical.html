Networking & Optical (AI-cluster interconnect)

Definition: The interconnect fabric that wires AI accelerators into a single training/
inference cluster — high-radix switches (Ethernet/InfiniBand), optical transceivers (800G→1.6T),
DSP/retimer silicon, and emerging co-packaged optics (CPO). As GPU counts per cluster explode,
interconnect bandwidth becomes the binding scaling constraint, making this the highest-growth
sub-layer within the AI buildout. The higher-beta expression of ai-infrastructure.
Conviction 4 on the trend-radar.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

Switch systems, switch/connectivity silicon, and optical components/modules connecting AI
accelerators. Scope spans the GPU-attached fabric (NVIDIA NVLink/InfiniBand/Spectrum-X), merchant
switch ASICs (Broadcom), optical modules (Coherent, Lumentum, InnoLight), DSP/retimers (Marvell,
Credo), and contract optical manufacturing (Fabrinet, Celestica).

4. Value-chain / linkage

Upstream lasers/photonic chips + DSP/SerDes IP + foundry → connectivity silicon (Broadcom
Tomahawk/Jericho switch ASICs, Marvell DSPs, Credo retimers) → optical components/modules
(Coherent, Lumentum, InnoLight) → contract manufacturing (Fabrinet, Celestica) → switch
systems (Arista, Cisco, NVIDIA) → AI clusters. Adjacent: ai-infrastructure (the compute
it connects), semiconductors (switch ASICs, DSP/SerDes), critical-minerals (China optical
supply concentration / geopolitics).

5. Competitive landscape

  • Switch silicon: concentrated — Broadcom (merchant switch ASICs) dominant; NVIDIA captures
    the GPU-attached fabric (InfiniBand + Spectrum-X) by bundling; Marvell in custom/DSP.
  • Switch systems: Arista + Cisco + NVIDIA the leaders; Arista the AI-Ethernet share winner at
    hyperscalers, Cisco re-accelerating via hyperscaler orders.
  • Optical components: fragmenting & competitive — Western names (Coherent, Lumentum) compete
    with Chinese module makers (InnoLight et al., reportedly 7 of top-10 module seats), but
    chip/silicon vendors capture the durable margin ("Chinese modules own the seats, chip companies
    make the money" — est.).
  • Moats: switch ASIC IP + software (Broadcom, Arista EOS); laser/photonics process know-how
    (Coherent, Lumentum); DSP/SerDes IP at advancing speeds (Marvell, Credo).
  • Margin map: software-rich switch systems ~62% GM (Arista); merchant connectivity silicon high
    (~78% Broadcom); optical modules structurally lower but rising (Lumentum GM ~34% and climbing).

7. Competitiveness read

Winning: Arista is the standout system winner — AI-fabric revenue doubling annually, FY26 guide
raised to ~$11.5B, with software-rich 62%+ gross margins pure-hardware peers can't match. Broadcom
wins twice (merchant switch ASICs and optical DSP), capturing silicon-layer margin regardless of
which system vendor or module maker ships. Lumentum and Coherent are clear cyclical winners on the
800G→1.6T transition (Lumentum +58% YoY, GM +600bps; Coherent DC&C +33.6%); Marvell is positioning
for the next leg via Celestial AI. NVIDIA quietly wins the attached fabric (DC networking +199%
YoY) by bundling Spectrum-X/InfiniBand with GPUs.

Pressured: Pure optical-module makers — especially Chinese volume leaders (InnoLight et al.) —
"own the seats but the chip companies make the money": they ship the units at thin, competitive
margins while DSP/laser-IP vendors keep the economics. Contract manufacturers (Fabrinet,
Celestica) ride the volume with structurally thin margins. Cisco is re-accelerating but its
non-GAAP GM slipped 260bps YoY on mix and memory costs.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Tightest coupling to AI capex: higher beta to hyperscaler spend than compute (networking
    growing faster) — a capex digestion or circular-financing unwind (see ai-infrastructure §8)
    hits interconnect hardest.
  • Architecture risk: copper-to-optical and CPO transitions could reshuffle winners; a slower
    CPO ramp, or NVIDIA bundling more fabric in-house, compresses the merchant opportunity.
  • Margin fragility in optical modules: thin GMs (Lumentum GAAP NI only $4.2M on $534M rev)
    leave little cushion; memory/component cost inflation flagged by Arista and Cisco.
  • China supply concentration / geopolitics: module supply chain heavily Chinese; tariffs/
    export controls cut both ways.
  • Valuation: ANET, COHR, LITE, CRDO trade at 42–60x forward — priced for sustained hypergrowth;
    any deceleration de-rates sharply (ANET "punished for perfection" despite +35%).

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 4/5 (calibrated — arguably better risk/reward within AI than raw compute because
    it's earlier on its S-curve, but it inherits all of ai-infrastructure's capex-dependency risk
    plus richer valuations; not a 5).
  • Stage: early→accelerating (800G ramping, 1.6T/CPO ahead — more runway than compute).
  • Horizon: 3–6 years.
  • Invalidation: NVIDIA DC-networking or Arista AI-fabric growth decelerating to sub-20% YoY for
    2+ quarters, OR a hyperscaler 2027 capex cut, OR a CPO/architecture shift that disintermediates
    merchant optical/switch vendors.
