Power Grid & Electrification

Definition: Utilities, grid equipment (transformers, switchgear, cables), electrical
distribution, and data-center power systems — the build-out and upgrade of the physical
electricity system, accelerated by AI data-center load. The most direct, cash-generative,
supply-constrained expression of the "AI needs power" thesis (ai-infrastructure). The
flagship sub-theme of energy-transition. Conviction 4 on the trend-radar.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

Generation owners (IPPs/utilities), transmission & distribution equipment OEMs (transformers,
switchgear, cables), data-center power/thermal systems, and the EPC contractors that install them.
Links to ai-infrastructure (demand), natural-gas-lng (turbines), nuclear-uranium
(baseload), critical-minerals (copper, grain-oriented electrical steel).

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: AI/data-center load (AI-specific DC demand +~50% in 2025; total DC power +17% in 2025,
Vertiv/industry); electrification of transport/heat; reshoring load. Supply (bullish for
equipment pricing): transformer lead times up to 4 years, cables 2–3 years (both ~doubled since
2021); transformer prices +~75% since ~2019 — supply scarcity = pricing power and expanding
margins. Bottlenecks: permitting, transformer/cable supply chains.

4. Value-chain / linkage

Raw materials (copper, grain-oriented electrical steel — critical-minerals) → transformer/
switchgear/cable OEMs (GE Vernova, Schneider, ABB, Siemens Energy, Eaton) → EPC/install (Quanta) →
data-center power/cooling (Vertiv, Eaton — data-center-cooling) → power supply (Constellation,
Vistra, NextEra). This is the monetization layer of ai-infrastructure: AI capex → power
demand → grid + on-site power. Ties to natural-gas-lng (GE Vernova gas-turbine backlog) and
reshoring-supply-chain/infrastructure-capex (the contractors).

5. Competitive landscape

Concentrated oligopoly in high-voltage equipment (GE Vernova, Siemens Energy, ABB, Schneider,
Hitachi) — long lead times + qualified-supplier lock-in = real moats and expanding margins.
Data-center power/thermal (Vertiv, Eaton) benefits from hyperscaler incumbency. IPPs with
nuclear/dispatchable fleets (Constellation, Vistra) hold scarce 24/7 clean power. EPC (Quanta) more
competitive but backlog-rich.

7. Competitiveness read

Winning: GE Vernova is the standout — Q1'26 revenue +16%, Electrification orders +86% organic
(book-to-bill ~2.5), $163B backlog, segment EBITDA margin jumped to 17.8%; gas-turbine scarcity
(100GW backlog) gives multi-year pricing power. Vertiv (FY25 $10.23B, +29% Q3, 20.4% adj op) and
Eaton (Electrical Americas backlog +44%, DC orders +240%) are direct AI-DC beneficiaries with
incumbency. Schneider (record €40B) and Siemens Energy (record €138B backlog) ride the same
supply-constrained equipment cycle. Constellation/Vistra monetize scarce 24/7 clean power via
hyperscaler PPAs.

Watch: Quanta/EPC face labor-cost and execution risk; valuations are rich. GEV's Q1'26 net
margin was flattered by a $4.5B Prolec M&A gain — the underlying 9.6% adj EBITDA is the real figure
(inference). Siemens Energy still carries wind (Gamesa) baggage.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • AI-capex disappointment / data-center demand re-rate (correlated with the whole
    ai-infrastructure complex; see theme-linkage-map).
  • Demand "air pocket" if hyperscaler power deals get cancelled/renegotiated.
  • Supply-chain easing compresses the transformer/cable pricing power driving margins.
  • Valuation: GEV at ~$280B prices in a lot; multiple-compression risk.
  • Rate/permitting/interconnection delays slow utility capex realization (IEA bottlenecks, fact).

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 4/5 — strongest, most direct monetization of AI power demand with supply-
    constrained pricing power and visible backlogs. Held below 5 by rich valuations and AI-capex
    correlation.
  • Stage: accelerating. Horizon: 3–7 years.
  • Invalidation: hyperscaler 2027 capex cut, OR GEV/Eaton backlog growth flattening YoY for 2+
    quarters, OR transformer lead times normalizing (signalling supply caught up).
