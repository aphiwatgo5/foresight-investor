Swarm intelligence

Definition: Collective, system-level behavior that emerges from the interactions of many
individual agents — none of which individually possesses the resulting "intelligence."

Summary

mirofish frames itself as a "swarm intelligence engine" that builds a mirror of reality
by capturing the collective emergence triggered by individual interactions. The claimed
advantage over traditional prediction is that macro outcomes arise bottom-up from
micro-level agent behavior rather than being modeled top-down (mirofish).

Key facts

  • Defined operationally as collective emergence from individual agent interaction. (mirofish)
  • Positioned as the differentiator vs. "traditional prediction." (mirofish)
  • Produced in practice via multi-agent-simulation. (mirofish)

Relationships

  • The emergent output of multi-agent-simulation; the core selling concept of mirofish.

Notes & tensions

  • "Swarm intelligence" here is used as a product framing; no formal model or metric of
    emergence is given in the source. (mirofish)
