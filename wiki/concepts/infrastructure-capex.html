Infrastructure & Capex Super-Cycle

Definition: The broad physical build-out — data centers, grid, transport, water, and federally-
funded infrastructure (IIJA) — and the building-products, aggregates, engineering, and equipment
companies that supply it. The "picks-and-shovels of everything physical": where the AI build-out
(ai-infrastructure, power-grid-electrification), reshoring (reshoring-supply-chain), and
public-infrastructure spending all converge into one capex wave. Conviction 4 on the trend-radar
— multiple stacked, durable demand sources hitting supply-constrained, high-moat building inputs.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

(a) Aggregates & building materials — Vulcan, Martin Marietta, CRH, Eagle Materials (the most
moat-rich layer: local quarry monopolies, pricing power); (b) engineering & construction / EPC —
Quanta, Jacobs, AECOM, Fluor (overlap reshoring-supply-chain); (c) building products / HVAC /
electrical — Trane, Johnson Controls, Eaton, Comfort Systems (overlap data-center-cooling,
power-grid-electrification); (d) equipment & rental — Caterpillar, United Rentals, Deere.
The convergence of AI-DC + grid + reshoring + public infra is what makes this a super-cycle.

3. Key structural drivers

Demand (stacked): AI data-center construction (ai-infrastructure, data-center-cooling);
grid build-out (power-grid-electrification); federal IIJA deployment (roads, bridges, broadband,
water); reshoring factory construction (reshoring-supply-chain). Supply (bullish for materials
pricing): aggregates are local, permitting-constrained, and effectively un-importable → durable
pricing power; skilled-labor scarcity limits EPC capacity. Headwinds: rate sensitivity of
private construction, residential-construction softness, and federal-funding/political timing.

4. Value-chain / linkage

Demand sources (AI-DC + grid + IIJA + reshoring) → engineering/design (Jacobs, AECOM) → EPC/construct
(Quanta, Fluor) using aggregates/cement (Vulcan, Martin Marietta, CRH) + equipment (Caterpillar,
United Rentals) + building products/HVAC/electrical (Trane, JCI, Eaton). Overlaps
power-grid-electrification, data-center-cooling, reshoring-supply-chain. Conditioned by
macro-regime (rates, fiscal policy). The aggregates layer is the highest-moat node in the chain.

5. Competitive landscape

Aggregates is the standout structure — local quarry monopolies (high weight-to-value makes
hauling uneconomic beyond ~50 miles), giving Vulcan and Martin Marietta near-unassailable regional
pricing power and decades of reserves. This is one of the best moat structures in all of industrials.
Building products / HVAC (Trane, Carrier, JCI) benefit from data-center thermal + electrification
demand with strong brands and installed bases. Equipment (Caterpillar, Deere, United Rentals) is
more cyclical but consolidated. EPC (Quanta, Jacobs, Fluor) is backlog-rich but more competitive/
execution-risk-prone. Moats and margins concentrate in aggregates and equipment/building products,
less in EPC.

7. Competitiveness read

The aggregates duopoly (Vulcan, Martin Marietta) is the highest-quality way to own the
super-cycle — local-monopoly pricing power, decades of reserves, and exposure to all the demand
waves at once (DC + grid + IIJA + reshoring) with no AI-specific concentration risk. Eaton and
Trane capture the electrical/thermal content of every data center and electrification project
(overlapping power-grid-electrification / data-center-cooling). Caterpillar and United
Rentals are the equipment beneficiaries (more cyclical). Quanta and Comfort Systems ride
record EPC/install backlogs but are labor-constrained.

Why conviction 4: the stacking of multiple durable demand sources onto supply-constrained,
high-moat inputs (especially aggregates) is unusually robust, and parts of it (IIJA, aggregates
pricing) are less AI-cyclical than the pure Compute themes — giving partial diversification. Held
below 5 by rate sensitivity, residential-construction drag, and the fact that the AI-linked portions
share the same de-rating risk as the rest of the capex complex (theme-linkage-map).

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Rates / recession: higher-for-longer rates cut private construction and equipment capex
    (macro-regime).
  • AI-capex digestion: the data-center-construction portion correlates with ai-infrastructure.
  • Federal funding timing: IIJA deployment delays or post-election policy shifts.
  • Residential drag: housing weakness offsets non-residential strength for materials/equipment.
  • Valuation: Quanta, Eaton, aggregates names have re-rated; rich multiples carry compression risk.

9. Investable instruments (candidates — not recommendations)

  • PAVE (Global X US Infrastructure Development) — the cleanest broad proxy (Quanta, Eaton, Vulcan, Trane).
  • XLI (Industrial Select Sector SPDR) — broad industrials.
  • IGF (iShares Global Infrastructure) — global infra tilt.
  • Representative securities: CAT, VMC, MLM, TT, ETN, PWR, URI, CRH.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 4/5 — multiple stacked, durable demand waves (AI-DC + grid + IIJA + reshoring)
    hitting supply-constrained, high-moat inputs (aggregates), with partial diversification from the
    pure-AI cycle. Held below 5 by rate sensitivity and the AI-linked portion's de-rating correlation.
  • Stage: accelerating. Horizon: 3–10 years.
  • Invalidation: US total construction spend and aggregates pricing both roll over for 2+ quarters,
    OR IIJA funding is materially clawed back, OR a rate shock freezes private construction.
