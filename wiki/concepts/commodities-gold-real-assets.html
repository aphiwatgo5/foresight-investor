Commodities, Gold & Real Assets

Definition: Hard assets as a hedge against currency debasement, inflation, and fiscal/
geopolitical risk — primarily gold (and gold miners), with broader commodities exposure.
Driven by record central-bank gold buying, fiscal-deficit/debasement fears, and de-dollarization.
After a powerful 2025 run to record highs (~$5,500+/oz) and a 2026 correction (~−17–18%), it reads
as cyclical-within-structural. Conviction 3.5 on the trend-radar — a genuine debasement hedge,
but post-parabolic and non-cash-generative.

1. Definition & scope

(a) Physical gold / ETFs — GLD, IAU (bullion exposure); (b) gold miners — Newmont (NEM),
Agnico Eagle (AEM), Barrick (GOLD), plus the GDX miners basket; (c) broad commodities —
energy, industrial metals (overlaps critical-minerals, natural-gas-lng). The unifying
thesis is real assets as a store of value against fiat debasement + a portfolio diversifier with
low correlation to the AI-equity complex.

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: record/sustained central-bank buying (~800t/yr, de-dollarization + reserve
diversification); fiscal-deficit/debasement fears (rising sovereign debt loads); geopolitical
hedging; and ETF/retail flows returning. Supply: gold mine supply grows slowly (~1–2%/yr),
making the central-bank + investment bid the price driver. Real-rate/USD direction is the swing
factor (gold competes with real yields — links to macro-regime). The 2026 correction reflects
profit-taking after a parabolic 2025, not a thesis break.

4. Value-chain / linkage

Miners (NEM/AEM/GOLD extract + leverage the gold price) → refiners → bullion/ETFs (GLD/IAU) →
central banks + investors (the structural bid). Gold is the low-correlation diversifier to the
AI-equity complex (ai-infrastructure, us-megacap-quality) and a debasement hedge tied to
macro-regime (deficits, USD, real rates). Broad commodities overlap critical-minerals
(copper/metals), natural-gas-lng (energy), and China demand (china-contrarian).

5. Competitive landscape

Bullion is undifferentiated (price-takers); the equity angle is gold miners, where cost
position + reserve quality + capital discipline differentiate. Agnico Eagle (low-cost, safe
jurisdictions) is the quality leader; Newmont (largest, more operational variability); Barrick
(turnaround/cost focus). Miners offer leveraged gold-price exposure but carry operational/
jurisdiction/capital-allocation risk. Moats: irreplaceable reserves + low all-in sustaining costs;
no moat in bullion itself.

7. Competitiveness read

Gold has done its job: a ~75% surge from early-2025 (~$2,624) to a January-2026 record (~$5,589+),
driven by a structural central-bank bid (~800t/yr), debasement/deficit fears, and geopolitical
hedging — a textbook debasement-hedge regime. The ~17–18% correction since (to ~$4,600 by May
2026) looks like a healthy post-parabolic reset rather than a thesis break, and analysts remain
constructive (Reuters poll median ~$4,747; JPMorgan ~$6,300 YE26). The structural drivers
(de-dollarization, sovereign-debt loads, low-correlation diversification away from the AI-equity
complex) remain intact. Among the equities, Agnico Eagle stands out for low-cost, safe-
jurisdiction quality; Newmont offers scale; Barrick adds copper optionality. The honest caveats:
gold is non-cash-generative (no yield/earnings), it just ran parabolic (entry timing matters),
and price levels here are search-aggregated and must be re-verified. Net: a legitimate structural
debasement hedge and diversifier — 3.5 — but post-run and best sized as portfolio insurance, not
a growth engine.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Real-rate/USD reversal — rising real yields + strong USD are gold's primary headwind (macro-regime).
  • Post-parabolic correction — after a +75% run, drawdowns can be sharp (2026 already −17–18%).
  • No yield/cash flow — gold's opportunity cost rises when real assets/equities compound.
  • Central-bank buying slowdown — if the structural bid fades, a key support weakens.
  • Miner-specific — cost inflation, jurisdiction/political risk, poor capital allocation.
  • Data risk — quoted levels are aggregated, not live; verify before acting.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 3.5/5 — a genuine, structurally-supported debasement hedge and rare low-correlation
    diversifier to the AI-equity complex, with a durable central-bank bid. Capped below 4 by its
    non-cash-generative nature, the just-completed parabolic run (timing risk), and data-verification
    caveats.
  • Stage: mature/structural with a cyclical correction underway post-2025 spike. Horizon: 3–7 years.
  • Invalidation: a sustained high-real-rate + strong-USD regime that breaks the debasement trade,
    OR a durable end to central-bank accumulation.
