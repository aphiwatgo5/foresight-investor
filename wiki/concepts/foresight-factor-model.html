Foresight Factor Model (FFM)

Definition: An independent 6-factor rating engine that produces A+/A/B+/.../F grades
and Strong Buy → Strong Sell ratings — WITHOUT using Seeking Alpha data as input. Built
specifically to address SA's documented blind spots using peer-reviewed factor research.
The "edge over SA" lives in the 6 upgrades (below).

Scope. Decisions for the operator's own book — not personalized advice. Independent
from SA (SA data is used only for benchmarking, never as input). All figures from
yfinance + macro + analyst events.

The 6 factors (and how each beats SA)

Factor What it measures SA equivalent FFM upgrade Evidence
Momentum Vol-scaled + idiosyncratic momentum SA Momentum (double-weighted, no vol scaling) Vol scaler cuts exposure to crash-prone names; idiosyncratic strips market beta Daniel-Moskowitz 2016: ~24% Sharpe lift
Value Sector-relative (70%) + absolute (30%), quality-gated SA Valuation (pure cheapness percentile) Quality gate: if GM < sector median → value score capped (kills value traps) Piotroski-style overlay
Quality Full QMJ: profitability + safety + payout SA Profitability (thin slice) Adds safety (low beta/leverage/vol) + payout dimensions SA omits Asness 2014 AQR QMJ
Growth Rev + EPS + margin trajectory + acceleration SA Growth Multi-period growth + acceleration bonus from quarterly fundamentals Standard growth factor
CMA Low asset-growth (Fama-French investment factor) NOT IN SA Low asset-growth = disciplined capital allocation; catches over-investment risk Fama-French 2015: ~27% explanatory power
Revisions 90d net-upgrade ratio + price-target delta SA Revisions Uses yfinance upgrades_downgrades (10y event log) — independent of SA Standard revisions factor

Composite rating engine

  • Regime-aware weights (the key structural advantage):
  • Bull: Momentum ×1.5, Value ×0.75 (ride the trend)
  • Bear: Value ×1.5, Quality ×1.25, Momentum ×0.5 (defensive)
  • Panic: Momentum ×0.25, Quality ×1.5 (crash protection)
  • Knockout gates (mirror SA's hard disqualifiers):
  • D+ or worse in Growth/Momentum/Revisions → cap at Hold
  • D- or worse in Value/Quality → cap at Sell
  • Output: 0-100 composite → Strong Buy (75+) / Buy (60+) / Hold (45+) / Sell (30+) / Strong Sell (<30)

Head-to-head vs SA (as of 2026-07-19)

  • 43 tickers with both FFM + SA grades
  • 70% rating agreement (within 1 level)
  • 30% major disagreements (2+ levels apart) — the research edge
  • FFM wins on: CCJ (−29%, FFM Sell vs SA Buy), LEU (−23%), IONQ (−22%), JNJ (+9%, FFM Strong Buy vs SA Hold), PANW (+115%, FFM Strong Buy vs SA Hold)
  • SA wins on: FTNT (+96%), INTC (+39%), DDOG (+110%), NET (+41%), OKTA (+107%) — momentum-driven runs FFM's vol-scaling undervalued

Current rating distribution (268 tickers)

Strong Buy: 14 · Buy: 46 · Hold: 89 · Sell: 111 · Strong Sell: 8

Known limitations

  • Revisions factor uses upgrades_downgrades (analyst actions), not EPS estimate revisions (paywalled at SA). Close proxy but not identical.
  • Growth factor uses point-in-time fundamentals for some inputs (quarterly history is only ~5 quarters deep from yfinance).
  • Momentum vol-scaling can be too conservative in strong bull runs (the SA wins above are mostly momentum runners FFM's vol-scaler dampened).
  • CMA penalizes capex-heavy growers (NVDA/MU get D-) — correct for asset-growth anomaly but misses the AI capex thesis rationale.
