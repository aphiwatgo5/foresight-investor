Data-Center Cooling

Definition: The thesis that thermal management is a structural bottleneck and high-growth
"pick-and-shovel" layer of the AI buildout. As GPU rack power densities blow past what air cooling
can handle, liquid cooling (direct-to-chip, immersion), CDUs, advanced HVAC, and power-and-
cooling infrastructure become mandatory — not optional. Insight only — not advice (trend-radar).

1. Definition & scope

  • Liquid cooling — direct-to-chip cold plates, rear-door heat exchangers, immersion; coolant
    distribution units (CDUs)    , manifolds, fluids. Now the default for new AI data-center designs
    (fact, Vertiv Q1 2026 commentary).
  • Thermal management / HVAC — chillers, CRAH/CRAC units, air handling, building thermal systems
    for the white-space + grey-space.
  • Mechanical / electrical install — specialty contractors building and fitting out the rooms
    (comfort-systems-usa).
  • Power + cooling integration — rack PDUs, busways, enclosures bundled with cooling
    (nvent-electric, vertiv).

2. Market size & growth

  • Data-center liquid-cooling market: estimates cluster around ~$4.7–6.8B in 2026, scaling to
    ~$18–28B by 2031–2035, at ~18–31% CAGR depending on scope (est., multiple market-research
    firms — Grand View, MarketsandMarkets, GMInsights, Mordor, 2026). Wide spread = methodology
    differences; directionally all show high-teens-to-30% CAGR.
  • Example point: $4.07B (2026) → $27.65B (2033) @ 31.5% CAGR (est., one 2026 forecast).
  • Example point: $6B (2026) → $27.1B (2035) @ 18.2% CAGR (est., GMInsights).
  • Demand evidence (hard order data): vertiv backlog $12.45B at Q1'26, +80.8% YoY (fact);
    nvent-electric entered 2026 with $2.3B backlog, ~3x prior year, built mostly from large
    hyperscale liquid-cooling orders (fact); comfort-systems-usa backlog $12.5B record
    (fact); johnson-controls backlog $13.3B, +22% YoY on data-center demand (fact).

3. Key structural drivers

Demand side:
- Rack power density — AI/GPU racks (e.g. NVIDIA GB-class) exceed air-cooling limits, forcing
liquid cooling as default (ai-infrastructure, semiconductors).
- Hyperscale capex super-cycle — orders/backlogs up 50–80% YoY across the group (Q1 2026).
- Efficiency / PUE + water pressure pushes liquid and closed-loop designs.

Supply side:
- Engineering + integration scarcity — full thermal-chain integration (chip-to-chiller) is hard;
incumbents with reference designs win share.
- Skilled-labor bottleneck for mechanical install (comfort-systems-usa flags labor as the
constraint, not demand).
- Margin expansion as mix shifts to higher-value liquid systems (Vertiv adj. op margin 20.8%,
+430bps; Comfort Systems gross margin 26.3% record).

4. Value-chain / linkage

  • → ai-infrastructure: cooling is a direct enabler of GPU compute density; gates how many
    chips fit per rack/room.
  • → semiconductors: higher TDP chips (ai-infrastructure GPUs/HBM) create the thermal
    problem — cooling demand is downstream of chip roadmaps.
  • → power-grid-electrification / natural-gas-lng / energy-transition: cooling + power
    are the two "physical layer" bottlenecks; both ride the same data-center capex.
  • Chain: chip TDP → server/rack design → liquid cooling (cold plate/CDU/manifold) → room HVAC /
    chillers → building → grid power.

5. Competitive landscape

  • Power-and-cooling platforms: vertiv is the closest pure-play scale leader (full thermal
  • power stack, reference designs with chipmakers); schneider-electric the diversified European
    giant (bought Motivair for liquid cooling); nvent-electric strong in liquid-cooling enclosures
  • PDUs.
  • Thermal components / coils: modine-manufacturing — coils/CDUs, fast-growing Climate
    Solutions segment.
  • HVAC OEM: johnson-controls — chillers + thermal systems, large installed base.
  • Specialty install (services): comfort-systems-usa — mechanical/electrical contractor;
    capacity-/labor-constrained, not demand-constrained.
  • Servers (adjacent): super-micro-computer — liquid-cooled rack-scale systems (DLC), but a
    server OEM with thin margins, not a cooling pure-play.
  • Structure: fragmented across components/install but consolidating around a few integrated
    platforms; moats sit in system integration + reference-design lock-in + backlog.

7. Competitiveness read

vertiv is the clearest quality compounder of the group: an integrated power + thermal
platform with a $12.45B backlog (+80.8% YoY), expanding margins (adj. op margin 20.8%, +430 bps),
and reference-design partnerships that lock it into hyperscale GPU rollouts. Its valuation (cap
~$113–121B on ~$13.5–14B FY26 sales) prices in years of that growth, so it carries the most
multiple-compression risk if AI capex digests. The component + HVAC names (modine-manufacturing
+47% with data-center revenue >$400M/qtr, nvent-electric +53% with a 3x backlog,
johnson-controls $13.3B backlog) offer cheaper, more diversified exposure — they participate in
the same demand without being priced as pure AI plays.

The install layer (comfort-systems-usa, +56% revenue, record 26.3% gross margin, $12.5B
backlog) is striking for margin expansion, but management explicitly flags skilled labor as the
binding constraint — a reminder the bottleneck is physical execution, not orders. schneider-electric
and super-micro-computer are partial expressions: Schneider is a diversified electrification giant
where cooling is one growth vector (Motivair), while Super Micro is a thin-margin server OEM riding
liquid-cooled racks rather than a cooling-IP owner.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • AI capex digestion / air-pocket — orders are the most cyclical part of the AI stack; a hyperscale
    capex pause hits backlogs fast (ai-infrastructure digestion risk).
  • Valuation — vertiv (and the group) re-rated hard; high bar already priced.
  • Commoditization of liquid-cooling hardware as competitors and chipmakers in-source designs.
  • Labor / supply-chain constraints cap conversion of backlog to revenue.
  • Technology shift — if chip efficiency or alternative cooling reduces liquid intensity.
  • Customer concentration — exposure to a handful of hyperscalers.

10. Conviction, stage, horizon

  • Conviction: 4/5 (inference) — demand visibility is exceptional (backlogs +20–80% YoY across the
    group; liquid cooling is now default). Held below 5 by stretched valuations and the segment's high
    beta to AI-capex cyclicality (it sits inside the correlated-AI-engine cluster, trend-radar).
  • Stage: accelerating (early-to-mid; liquid-cooling adoption inflecting).
  • Horizon: 2–5 years (tracks the data-center build cycle closely).
  • Invalidation: hyperscale capex orders roll over / backlog growth turns negative for 2+ quarters
    → step down to 2–3.
