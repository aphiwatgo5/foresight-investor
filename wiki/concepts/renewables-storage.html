Renewables & Storage

Definition: Solar, wind, and — most importantly for the investment case — battery energy
storage systems (BESS): the fastest-to-deploy, increasingly cost-competitive supply that helps
meet surging electricity demand (power-grid-electrification, ai-infrastructure). A core
sub-theme of energy-transition, but policy- and rate-sensitive and exposed to Chinese
manufacturing oversupply. Conviction 3 on the trend-radar — storage is the bright spot;
solar/wind economics are mixed.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

Three layers: (a) generation — utility-scale + distributed solar (First Solar, Enphase),
onshore/offshore wind (Vestas, Ørsted, GE Vernova wind); (b) storage — grid-scale BESS
(Tesla Megapack, Fluence) and residential storage (Enphase, Tesla Powerwall); (c) developers/
utilities with large renewable pipelines (NextEra). Storage is the highest-conviction layer — it
is the flexibility that makes the grid (and AI load) work and is growing fastest.

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: electricity-demand surge (AI/data centers, electrification) needs both new generation
and flexibility; solar is the cheapest new generation in most markets; BESS solves intermittency and
provides grid services (frequency, capacity, arbitrage). Supply/cost: battery pack prices at
record lows (~$110/kWh) on Chinese overcapacity — great for storage deployers, brutal for
manufacturers' margins. Headwinds: US policy/IRA-credit uncertainty, tariffs on Chinese
cells/modules, high rates pressuring project IRRs, interconnection-queue delays.

4. Value-chain / linkage

critical-minerals (lithium, copper) → cells/modules (largely China: CATL, BYD) → BESS
integrators (Tesla, Fluence) / inverters (Enphase, SolarEdge) / panels (First Solar) → developers &
utilities (NextEra) → power-grid-electrification → serves ai-infrastructure load. Complements
nuclear-uranium and natural-gas-lng (firm vs intermittent power mix). Tightly coupled to
interest rates and policy (macro-regime).

5. Competitive landscape

Solar manufacturing is brutal and commoditized — Chinese oversupply has crushed module prices
and margins; First Solar is the exception (US thin-film, IRA-advantaged, differentiated tech,
defensible US manufacturing). Inverters/residential (Enphase, SolarEdge) are cyclical and
rate-sensitive, having de-rated hard. Storage integration (Tesla Energy, Fluence) is the
healthiest layer — value sits in software/integration and offtake, not cells. Wind is
structurally challenged (Ørsted writedowns, Siemens Gamesa losses) outside select players.
Utilities/developers (NextEra) hold the scale, pipeline, and financing moat.

7. Competitiveness read

The storage layer is the high-conviction part of this theme: record-low battery costs plus the
flexibility the grid needs make BESS the fastest-growing energy segment, and it directly serves the
AI-load build-out (power-grid-electrification). First Solar stands out on the generation
side — IRA-advantaged US manufacturing, differentiated thin-film, sold-out backlog — a genuine moat
in an otherwise commoditized solar world. NextEra is the blue-chip way to own the whole
renewables+storage pipeline with a financing edge.

Weak/avoid: Chinese module oversupply has gutted solar-manufacturer economics; wind (Ørsted
impairments, Gamesa losses) remains structurally troubled; inverter/residential names (Enphase,
SolarEdge) are rate-sensitive and have de-rated. This is why the theme is conviction 3, not 4: the
demand is real but the equity quality is bifurcated and policy/rate-exposed.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Policy: IRA tax-credit repeal/phase-down or tariff shifts reprice US project economics.
  • Rates: high interest rates compress renewable-project IRRs and developer valuations.
  • Chinese oversupply: continued module/cell glut keeps manufacturer margins depressed.
  • Interconnection bottlenecks: queue delays slow deployment (power-grid-electrification).
  • Commodity swings: lithium/copper price moves (critical-minerals) hit storage economics.

  • TAN (Invesco Solar) — solar-focused.
  • GRID (First Trust Smart Grid Infra) — grid + storage tilt (also in power-grid-electrification).
  • LIT (Global X Lithium & Battery Tech) — battery supply chain.
  • Representative securities: NEE, FSLR, TSLA, ENPH, FLNC, CATL, VWS.CO.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 3/5 — storage is a genuine high-growth, cost-curve-driven bright spot, but the
    theme overall is dragged down by commoditized solar manufacturing, troubled wind, and heavy
    policy/rate sensitivity. Held below the AI-equipment themes for those reasons.
  • Stage: accelerating (storage). Horizon: 3–10 years.
  • Invalidation: US IRA credits repealed without replacement, OR battery-cost declines stall and
    storage-deployment growth flattens for 2+ quarters, OR sustained high rates freeze project pipelines.
