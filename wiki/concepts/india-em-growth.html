India & EM Growth

Definition: India as the structural growth engine of emerging markets — the fastest-growing
major economy (~6–7% GDP), a young demographic, a digital-public-infrastructure stack (UPI,
Aadhaar), and a manufacturing-incentive push (PLI). Played via large-cap leaders (Reliance,
HDFC/ICICI banks, Bharti Airtel, Infosys/TCS) and ADRs. Conviction 4 on the trend-radar —
one of the cleanest multi-decade structural-growth stories, tempered by valuation and a 2026
pullback.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

(a) Financials — HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank (private-bank credit-cycle leaders); (b) conglomerate/
energy — Reliance Industries; (c) telecom/digital — Bharti Airtel (UPI/data); (d) IT
services — Infosys, TCS (global outsourcing); (e) consumer — ITC. The unifying thesis is a
domestically-driven, demographically-powered compounding economy less exposed to global trade
shocks than export-led EMs.

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: young, urbanizing population + rising middle-class consumption; digital-public-infra
(UPI payments scale — links to fintech-payments); financial deepening (credit penetration
low, private banks gaining share); infrastructure build-out. Supply: "China+1" supply-chain
diversification + the PLI scheme drawing manufacturing FDI (links to reshoring-supply-chain);
a deep IT-services export engine (Infosys/TCS); capital-market reforms + strong domestic SIP
inflows providing a structural equity bid.

4. Value-chain / linkage

A largely domestic-demand economy: banks (HDFC/ICICI) fund consumption + capex → corporates
(Reliance, Bharti) build digital/energy/retail → consumers (ITC) → IT services (Infosys/TCS)
earn global FX. UPI ties to fintech-payments; manufacturing incentives to
reshoring-supply-chain (China+1). Currency/Fed-rate sensitivity to macro-regime (EM
flows track USD/US rates). Rotation alternative to us-megacap-quality and
china-contrarian.

5. Competitive landscape

Sector oligopolies with strong domestic moats: private banking consolidating around HDFC + ICICI
(scale, deposit franchises, low-cost CASA); telecom a 3-player market (Bharti gaining); IT
services globally competitive (TCS/Infosys cost + scale); Reliance a cross-sector conglomerate
(energy→telecom→retail). Moats: regulatory/licensing, distribution scale, brand, and — for IT —
global delivery cost advantage. Domestic orientation insulates from global-trade shocks.

7. Competitiveness read

India is the standout multi-decade structural-growth story in EM — a young demographic,
domestically-driven demand (less trade-shock exposed), digital-public-infra scale, financial
deepening, and a "China+1" manufacturing tailwind, all backed by a structural domestic-SIP
equity bid. The quality core is the private banks (HDFC, ICICI) — secular credit-penetration
winners — plus Reliance (digital/retail re-rating) and the IT majors (global, cash-rich,
pivoting to AI services). The caution is valuation: at ~20x earnings India rarely looks
cheap, and 2026 saw a meaningful pullback (Nifty off ~10% from its January ATH, ~−$533B market
cap) on global risk-off + foreign outflows. Net: among geographies this is the highest-conviction
structural grower — the question is entry valuation and FX timing, not the long-run thesis.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Valuation — persistently premium multiples (~20x) leave little margin; pullbacks can be sharp.
  • Foreign-flow volatility — EM-flow sensitivity to USD/US rates (macro-regime); 2026 outflows.
  • Currency (INR) depreciation eroding USD-denominated ADR returns.
  • Execution on manufacturing/PLI — China+1 gains could underdeliver vs hype.
  • Banking credit cycle — rapid loan growth carries asset-quality risk.
  • Political/policy continuity and reform pace.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 4/5 — one of the few geographies with a genuine multi-decade structural-growth
    runway (demographics + financial deepening + digital infra + China+1), and the cleanest EM
    diversifier away from US concentration. Held below 5 by premium valuation and EM-flow/FX
    volatility (the 2026 pullback is a live reminder).
  • Stage: accelerating (structural). Horizon: 5–10+ years.
  • Invalidation: a structural growth-rate downshift (GDP sustainably <5%), OR a banking
    asset-quality blowup, OR durable foreign-capital flight on a strong-USD regime.
