Quantum Computing

Definition: The earliest-stage, highest-variance frontier theme — quantum-computing pure-plays
(IonQ, Rigetti, D-Wave, Quantum Computing Inc.) and big-tech quantum programs (IBM, Google,
Microsoft). Tiny revenues against extreme valuations (P/S ratios in the hundreds), defined by
science-milestone risk and a long, uncertain path to commercial utility. Conviction 2 on the
trend-radar — genuine long-run optionality, but the most speculative theme in the radar.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

(a) Pure-plays — IonQ (IONQ, trapped-ion), Rigetti (RGTI, superconducting), D-Wave (QBTS,
annealing), Quantum Computing Inc. (QUBT, photonic); (b) big-tech programs — IBM, Google
(GOOGL — Willow chip), Microsoft (MSFT — Majorana/topological). The pure-plays offer concentrated
exposure at extreme valuations; big tech offers quantum optionality embedded in profitable
businesses (ai-software-agents, us-megacap-quality).

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: potential to solve classically-intractable problems (cryptography, materials/drug
discovery, optimization, logistics); government + defense funding; cloud-quantum access lowering
adoption friction; "quantum-readiness" / post-quantum-cryptography urgency (links to
cybersecurity). Supply/science: steady qubit-count + error-correction progress (Google
Willow, IBM roadmaps, Microsoft topological qubits); but fault-tolerant, commercially-useful
quantum advantage remains years away — the gating constraint on the whole theme.

4. Value-chain / linkage

Hardware/qubit platforms (IonQ ions, Rigetti/Google superconducting, QBTS annealing, QUBT
photonic) → error-correction/software/middleware → cloud access (IBM, AWS Braket, Azure Quantum) →
end-use (cryptography, simulation, optimization). Key cross-link to cybersecurity
(post-quantum cryptography / "harvest-now-decrypt-later" risk) and to ai-software-agents /
us-megacap-quality (big-tech embeds quantum optionality). Funding/risk-appetite sensitive to
macro-regime.

5. Competitive landscape

Pre-commercial and platform-fragmented — competing qubit modalities (trapped-ion,
superconducting, photonic, annealing, topological) with no clear winner, making it a
"which-technology-wins" bet on top of a "when-does-it-work" bet. Pure-plays (IONQ/RGTI/QBTS/QUBT)
are tiny, cash-burning, and extravagantly valued; big tech (IBM/Google/Microsoft) has vastly
deeper resources + research depth and can absorb a long R&D runway. Moats are prospective (IP,
qubit-quality leadership); none is durable yet.

7. Competitiveness read

Quantum is the most speculative theme in the radar — a genuine, potentially transformational
long-run technology priced as if commercialization were near, when fault-tolerant, useful quantum
advantage is still years away. The pure-plays illustrate the disconnect: IonQ is the revenue
leader ($64.67M, +755%, FY26 guide $260–270M) yet trades at ~109x sales, while Rigetti (~836x) and
D-Wave (~791x) carry valuations one analyst aptly called "historically unsustainable." On top of
the when-does-it-work risk sits a which-modality-wins risk (ion vs superconducting vs photonic
vs annealing vs topological), with no clear winner. The most defensible exposure is arguably the
big-tech programs (IBM, Google's Willow, Microsoft's topological bet) — quantum optionality
embedded inside profitable businesses that can fund a decade of R&D without existential dilution.
Net: real optionality, but extreme valuation + binary science risk + no durable moats justify a 2
— a tiny, lottery-ticket sleeve at most, with big-tech the lower-risk way to hold the call option.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Valuation — P/S in the hundreds leaves no margin; sentiment reversals are brutal.
  • Science/timeline risk — fault-tolerant, useful quantum advantage may remain years/decades out.
  • Modality risk — backing the wrong qubit technology.
  • Cash burn + dilution — pure-plays are not self-funding; capital-access dependent.
  • Big-tech crowd-out — IBM/Google/Microsoft could dominate, marginalizing pure-plays.
  • Risk-off macro (macro-regime) closing the speculative-funding window.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 2/5 — a potentially transformational frontier with real long-run optionality, but
    the most speculative theme in the radar: tiny revenues, extreme (hundreds-of-x) valuations, binary
    science/timeline risk, modality uncertainty, and no durable moats. Big-tech programs are the
    lower-risk way to hold the option.
  • Stage: emerging / frontier (extreme variance). Horizon: 7–10+ years.
  • Invalidation: repeated failure to progress error-correction toward useful advantage + a
    funding freeze for pure-plays; conversely, a credible fault-tolerance breakthrough would re-rate
    the theme.
