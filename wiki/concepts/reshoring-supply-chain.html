Reshoring & Supply-Chain Reconfiguration

Definition: The structural relocation of manufacturing and supply chains toward the US, Mexico,
India, and allied/"friend-shored" geographies — driven by tariffs, industrial policy (CHIPS Act,
IRA), geopolitical de-risking from China, and resilience priorities. The demand engine behind the
Reindustrialization & Defense meta-force, feeding infrastructure-capex, automation-robotics,
and power-grid-electrification. Conviction 3 on the trend-radar — a real multi-decade
re-wiring, but lumpy, policy-dependent, and partly already priced into industrials.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

Not a single industry but a demand wave that lands across: (a) construction/engineering & EPC
(factory and fab build-out — Jacobs, AECOM, Fluor); (b) electrical & industrial equipment
(Eaton, Rockwell — overlaps power-grid-electrification / automation-robotics); (c)
logistics/warehousing & industrial REITs; (d) beneficiary geographies — Mexico (nearshoring),
India (india-em-growth), Vietnam/ASEAN (asean-thailand). The cleanest investable expressions
are the contractors and equipment makers building the new capacity.

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: tariffs and trade policy (US-China decoupling, 2025 tariff escalation); industrial
policy subsidies (CHIPS for semiconductors fabs, IRA for clean-tech/batteries); supply-chain
resilience after COVID/geopolitical shocks; AI-data-center build-out itself is a reshoring-style
domestic capex wave (ai-infrastructure, power-grid-electrification). Enablers:
automation makes higher-cost-geography manufacturing viable (automation-robotics); allied
"friend-shoring" to India/Mexico/ASEAN. Headwinds: higher costs, skilled-labor shortages, long
build timelines, and policy reversibility.

4. Value-chain / linkage

Policy/tariffs → capex decisions → EPC/construction (Jacobs, Fluor, AECOM, Quanta) + electrical
equipment (Eaton, power-grid-electrification) + automation (automation-robotics) →
new fabs/plants/grid → output. Directly drives infrastructure-capex and overlaps
power-grid-electrification (new plants need power) and semiconductors (CHIPS fabs).
Geographic beneficiaries: india-em-growth, asean-thailand, Mexico. Strongly conditioned by
macro-regime (trade policy, rates).

5. Competitive landscape

This is a diffuse theme captured mostly through industrials, so moats vary: EPC/engineering
firms (Jacobs, Fluor, AECOM) are backlog-driven but competitive and execution-risk-prone;
electrical/industrial equipment (Eaton, Rockwell, Quanta) have the strongest moats and overlap
the power/automation themes (where the real pricing power sits). Industrial REITs / logistics
(Prologis) benefit from supply-chain reconfiguration with durable real-asset moats. There is no
single "reshoring champion" — the theme is better played via the equipment and contractor layers
that also appear in power-grid-electrification and infrastructure-capex.

7. Competitiveness read

The strongest way to own reshoring is through the equipment and EPC layers that also carry pricing
power in adjacent themes — Eaton (electrical content of every new plant and data center,
backlog +44%, 25.6% margins) and Quanta (grid/infrastructure EPC, record $48.5B backlog) are the
highest-quality expressions, doubling as power-grid-electrification plays. Engineering/EPC
firms (Jacobs for advanced facilities/fabs, AECOM, Fluor) ride the factory-construction boom but face
execution and competitive-bidding risk. Industrial REITs (Prologis) and aggregates (Vulcan)
offer durable real-asset exposure to supply-chain reconfiguration and the build-out.

Why conviction 3: the factory-construction boom is real and well-evidenced (US manufacturing
construction ~2x since 2021), but the theme is lumpy, policy-dependent (tariffs/CHIPS/IRA can
reverse), and largely expressed through industrials already re-rated on the same AI/power capex
wave — so it offers less incremental edge than the equipment themes it overlaps.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Policy reversal: repeal/rollback of CHIPS/IRA incentives or tariff de-escalation slows capex.
  • Execution/labor: skilled-labor shortages and cost overruns delay project completion.
  • Cyclicality: higher rates / recession cut industrial capex (macro-regime).
  • Overlap/crowding: best names already priced on the AI/power capex narrative — limited
    independent upside.
  • Geographic competition: Mexico/India/ASEAN capture share the US hoped to reshore.

9. Investable instruments (candidates — not recommendations)

  • PAVE (Global X US Infrastructure Development) — the cleanest broad proxy (Quanta, Eaton, Vulcan).
  • XLI (Industrial Select Sector SPDR) — broad industrials.
  • FLM / industrial-construction ETFs — construction tilt.
  • Representative securities: ETN, PWR, J, ACM, PLD, VMC.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 3/5 — a genuine multi-decade supply-chain re-wiring with hard evidence (factory
    construction boom), but lumpy, policy-reversible, and mostly expressed via already-re-rated
    industrials that overlap power-grid-electrification / infrastructure-capex.
  • Stage: accelerating. Horizon: 3–10 years.
  • Invalidation: US manufacturing-construction spend rolls over for 2+ quarters, OR a major tariff/
    industrial-policy reversal removes the subsidy catalyst.
