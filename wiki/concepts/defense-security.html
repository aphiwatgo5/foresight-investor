Defense & Security

Definition: Defense primes, missiles/munitions, military aerospace, and the new "defense-tech"
cohort (autonomous systems, software-defined warfare, space/ISR). Driven by a structural global
rearmament cycle — NATO spending hikes, the Ukraine/Middle East wars, and US-China strategic
competition. The flagship of the Reindustrialization & Defense meta-force, linking to
reshoring-supply-chain, automation-robotics, and space-economy. Conviction 4 on the
trend-radar — multi-year, policy-backed budget visibility with relatively low AI-capex
correlation = a diversifier from the Compute & AI engine.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

(a) Primes / platforms — Lockheed Martin, RTX, Northrop Grumman, General Dynamics, BAE Systems,
Rheinmetall; (b) munitions/missiles — the supply-constrained reload cycle (RTX, Lockheed,
Rheinmetall artillery); (c) defense-tech / software-defined — Palantir (software,
ai-software-agents), Anduril (private, autonomous systems), AeroVironment (drones); (d)
space/ISR & electronics. The investment story splits between the steady, backlog-rich primes
and the higher-growth, higher-multiple defense-tech disruptors.

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: Russia-Ukraine war (munitions reload, air defense); Middle East conflict; US-China
competition (Indo-Pacific, Taiwan); NATO 5%-GDP pledge and European rearmament (Germany,
Poland, Nordics); munitions-stockpile depletion driving multi-year reorders. Supply
(bullish): munitions/artillery capacity is supply-constrained — primes are expanding plants
(reshoring-supply-chain, infrastructure-capex); skilled labor and long-lead components limit
ramp = backlog and pricing power. Defense-tech tailwind: shift to autonomous/software-defined
systems (drones, AI targeting) reshaping procurement toward newer entrants.

4. Value-chain / linkage

critical-minerals / specialty materials + semiconductors (defense electronics) → components
& subsystems → primes/integrators (LMT, RTX, NOC, GD, Rheinmetall) → governments. Defense-tech
overlaps ai-software-agents (Palantir, AI/autonomy) and space-economy (ISR, launch).
Capacity expansion ties to reshoring-supply-chain / infrastructure-capex and
automation-robotics (factory automation for munitions). Demand is geopolitics-driven, largely
decoupled from the AI-capex cycle — its key diversification value (see theme-linkage-map).

5. Competitive landscape

A concentrated oligopoly of primes with deep moats: program lock-in (decade-long platforms),
classified/clearance barriers, certification, and government relationships make share extremely
sticky. Margins are steady (cost-plus + fixed-price mix) with massive multi-year backlogs.
Munitions is a near-duopoly/oligopoly per category with current pricing power from scarcity.
Defense-tech disruptors (Palantir, Anduril) attack the software/autonomy layer where primes are
weaker — Palantir's growth + margins are exceptional but its valuation is extreme (~40–50x sales).
Europe (Rheinmetall, BAE, Leonardo, Thales) is re-rating hard on the rearmament pledge.

7. Competitiveness read

Two winning archetypes. (1) Primes with backlog + pricing power — RTX and Lockheed
(missiles/air-defense reload), Northrop (B-21, space), General Dynamics (submarines) offer steady,
visible, multi-year revenue with deep moats and modest valuations relative to the AI names; the
munitions reload cycle is the cleanest near-term driver. (2) European rearmament —
Rheinmetall is the standout, compounding 30%+ growth on artillery/ammo scarcity and the NATO
5%-pledge; BAE also at record backlog. (3) Defense-tech — Palantir is the highest-growth,
highest-margin software-defined-warfare play, but its ~40–50x sales valuation prices in years of
execution and carries the most multiple-compression risk.

Why conviction 4: durable, policy-backed, multi-year budget visibility with low correlation to
the AI-capex cycle makes this a genuine diversifier (theme-linkage-map). Held below 5 by
program-execution/cost-overrun risk, political budget swings, and stretched defense-tech multiples.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Geopolitical de-escalation: a durable Ukraine/Middle East peace would slow the reload cycle
    (though structural NATO rearmament likely persists).
  • Budget politics: US continuing-resolution/sequestration risk; European fiscal constraints
    capping the pledge's realization.
  • Program execution: cost overruns, delays (F-35 sustainment, B-21 ramp) hit margins.
  • Defense-tech valuation: Palantir / disruptor multiples are extreme — any growth slip de-rates
    sharply.
  • Supply chain: munitions ramp constrained by labor and long-lead components.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 4/5 — multi-year, policy-backed rearmament super-cycle with deep prime moats and
    low AI-capex correlation (diversifier). Held below 5 by budget-politics, program-execution risk,
    and rich defense-tech valuations.
  • Stage: accelerating. Horizon: 3–10 years.
  • Invalidation: a durable broad peace settlement collapses the reload cycle AND NATO members walk
    back the 5% pledge, OR US defense budget is cut in real terms for 2+ consecutive years.
