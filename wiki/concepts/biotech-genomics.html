Biotech & Genomics

Definition: Large- and mid-cap biotech innovators (Vertex, Regeneron, Gilead, Amgen), the curative
gene/cell-therapy frontier (CRISPR/base editing — CRSP, NTLA, BEAM; approved Casgevy), and the
genomics-tools picks-and-shovels layer (Illumina sequencing). Context: XBI/IBB, a ~$300B patent cliff
driving M&A, and GLP-1 spillover capital. Conviction 3 on the trend-radar — powerful innovation,
but lumpy and binary at the platform level.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

(a) Profitable large-cap innovators — Vertex (CF, Casgevy, non-opioid pain), Regeneron
(Eylea/Dupixent), Gilead (HIV), Amgen; (b) gene/cell-therapy platforms — CRISPR Therapeutics,
Intellia, Beam (binary, clinical-stage); (c) genomics tools — Illumina (sequencing razor-and-blade).
M&A is the connective tissue linking small innovators to big-pharma balance sheets ahead of the patent
cliff.

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: aging + chronic-disease burden; curative one-time therapies (Casgevy) command very high
per-patient prices. Supply/innovation: maturing CRISPR/base-editing moving to pivotal trials/
approvals; falling sequencing costs (Illumina). Capital cycle: the ~$300B patent cliff forces big
pharma to acquire innovation → supports mid-cap valuations and M&A premia; GLP-1 cash (glp1-obesity-metabolic)
redeployed into bolt-ons.

4. Value-chain / linkage

Tools/instruments (Illumina sequencers, reagents) → platform biotechs (CRSP/NTLA/BEAM translate editing
into therapies) → commercial-stage innovators (Vertex, Regeneron, Gilead) fund pipelines and in-license/
acquire → big pharma as ultimate acquirer/distributor. Linkage to glp1-obesity-metabolic (capital
flows) and healthcare-aging-services (demand).

5. Competitive landscape

Bifurcated: a profitable large-cap tier with deep moats (Vertex CF monopoly, Regeneron antibody
platform, Gilead HIV franchise) vs a cash-burning clinical-stage tier where value is binary on readouts.
Moats: franchise/IP durability; platform IP + manufacturing know-how for cell/gene therapy; Illumina's
installed base + consumables razor-and-blade. M&A optionality is itself a value driver for the mid-cap tier.

7. Competitiveness read

Among large caps, Vertex and Regeneron look strongest on durable franchises and disciplined growth
(+8% and +19%), though Regeneron's net income dipped ~10% YoY on mix; Gilead is the steadiest cash
generator (HIV-led beat, raised guidance). In gene editing, CRSP is "winning" by being commercial
(Casgevy), but the revenue ramp is slow ($43M/quarter shared) — the science works faster than it
monetizes. NTLA and BEAM are pre-revenue, event-driven, with defining 2026 catalysts (NTLA HAE Ph3
mid-year; BEAM possible SCD filing). Illumina is a recovery story — guidance raised after difficult
years, but the tools market is cyclical with intensifying competition. Net: large caps are the safe
compounders; editors are high-variance optionality; Illumina is the cyclical re-rating.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Clinical/binary risk — editors live or die on single readouts.
  • Reimbursement/durability of multimillion-dollar one-time cures.
  • Patent cliffs hit large caps too (Regeneron Eylea biosimilar pressure).
  • Funding environment — clinical-stage biotech is rate-/sentiment-sensitive; XBI drawdowns shut off capital.
  • Tools cyclicality — Illumina tied to research/pharma capex budgets.
  • Manufacturing/scale-up complexity caps cell-and-gene-therapy revenue ramps.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 3/5 — powerful long-run innovation + supportive M&A/patent-cliff backdrop, but
    returns are lumpy and binary at the platform level, and the gene-therapy revenue ramp has lagged the
    science. Strong large caps alone would justify a 4; the speculative editing/genomics core that defines
    the upside holds it at 3.
  • Stage: emerging (editing) to mature (large-cap biotech). Horizon: 3–10 years.
  • Invalidation: a string of failed pivotal readouts (NTLA/BEAM) + a frozen biotech funding window,
    OR Illumina losing its sequencing installed-base lead.
