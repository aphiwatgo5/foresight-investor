Japan Corporate Reform

Definition: The structural re-rating of Japanese equities driven by corporate-governance
reform (TSE's "PBR >1x" campaign, record buybacks, unwinding cross-shareholdings), the end of
deflation (BOJ exiting negative rates), and foreign endorsement (Berkshire's stakes in the
five trading houses). Played via the trading houses (sogo shosha), banks, and global exporters.
Conviction 4 on the trend-radar — a genuine multi-year governance/reflation regime change,
with hedged-vs-unhedged FX the key implementation choice.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: foreign-investor re-engagement (Berkshire holds >10% of all five trading houses —
a powerful endorsement); the TSE "name-and-shame" PBR >1x campaign forcing capital return +
efficiency; TOPIX index reconstitution (through 2028) rewarding higher free-float/liquidity.
Supply (of returns): record buybacks (~¥960B), dividend hikes, and unwinding of cross-
shareholdings releasing capital; the end of deflation (BOJ at 0.75%, a ~30-year high) lifting
bank net-interest margins and nominal growth. Weak yen (~159) boosts exporter earnings.

4. Value-chain / linkage

Governance reform is the cross-cutting driver raising ROE/payouts across sectors: trading
houses (commodity/global diversified), banks (8306 — reflation/rate beneficiary), exporters
(Toyota, Sony) levered to weak yen + global demand, and Tokyo Electron tying directly to the
semiconductors capex cycle. FX (USD/JPY) is the key linkage to macro-regime — and the
reason hedged vs unhedged ETF choice matters for USD-based investors. Rotation alternative to
us-megacap-quality; regional peer to india-em-growth, asean-thailand.

5. Competitive landscape

Mature, scaled incumbents now competing on capital efficiency rather than growth: the five
trading houses are diversified global investment/commodity platforms (Berkshire-endorsed);
megabanks (MUFG) are reflation plays; exporters (Toyota, Sony, Tokyo Electron) hold genuine
global moats. The reform "edge" is governance-driven — buybacks, higher ROE, unwinding
cross-holdings — improving shareholder returns from a low base rather than creating new moats.

7. Competitiveness read

Japan is a genuine regime change, not a trade: after three decades of deflation and
capital-inefficient balance sheets, the combination of the TSE's PBR>1x governance campaign,
record buybacks (~¥960B), unwinding cross-shareholdings, and the BOJ's exit from negative rates
(0.75%, a 30-year high) is structurally re-rating the market — the Nikkei hit an all-time high
(~62,834) and PBR/ROE are rising (1.1→1.4x; 8.4→9.0%). Berkshire's >10% stakes in all five
trading houses are the marquee endorsement. The trading houses and megabanks (MUFG) are the
clearest reform/reflation beneficiaries; exporters (Toyota, Sony) and Tokyo Electron add global
cyclical leverage. The big caveat for USD-based investors is currency: a weak yen (~159)
flatters local returns but erodes unhedged USD returns — making the hedged-vs-unhedged ETF
choice the single most important implementation decision. Net: a high-quality, multi-year
diversifier away from US concentration, with the reform runway maturing but not finished.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Currency — a strengthening yen would hurt exporter earnings; a weak yen erodes unhedged
    USD returns (hedged-vs-unhedged is decisive) (macro-regime).
  • Reform fatigue — governance gains could plateau once the easy buybacks are done.
  • BOJ policy error — too-fast tightening could choke the reflation; too-slow risks the yen.
  • Global-demand/cyclical exposure (exporters, trading houses, semicap).
  • Demographic headwind — shrinking/aging population caps domestic growth long-term.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 4/5 — a real, multi-year governance + reflation regime change with strong
    external validation (Berkshire) and a structural re-rating runway, plus a valuable diversifier
    away from US-megacap concentration. Held below 5 by FX dependence, cyclical exposure, and the
    long-term demographic ceiling.
  • Stage: accelerating, now maturing. Horizon: 3–7 years.
  • Invalidation: a reversal of the TSE governance push / buyback slowdown, OR a sharp yen
    appreciation + BOJ policy error that breaks the reflation narrative.
