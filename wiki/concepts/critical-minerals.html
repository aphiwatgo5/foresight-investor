Critical Minerals

Definition: The thesis that the materials needed for electrification, semiconductors, and
defense — copper, lithium, rare earths (NdPr, Dy, Tb), and minor metals like gallium/germanium —
face a structural supply bottleneck against demand pulled forward by the grid, EVs, AI data
centers, and re-armament, compounded by extreme geographic concentration (China). Insight only —
not advice (trend-radar).

1. Definition & scope

  • Copper — the "metal of electrification": grids, motors, data centers, EVs
    (freeport-mcmoran, southern-copper, bhp, rio-tinto, glencore).
  • Lithium — battery cathode/energy storage (albemarle).
  • Rare earths — NdPr (magnets), Dy/Tb (heavy REE for high-temp magnets) for EV motors, wind,
    defense (mp-materials, lynas-rare-earths).
  • Minor / strategic metals — gallium, germanium (semis, optics, defense); antimony, graphite.
  • Scope note: "uranium-adjacent" overlaps energy-transition (uranium/nuclear) — tracked there.

2. Market size & growth

  • Copper supply gap: IEA warns of a ~30% supply deficit by 2035 under Stated Policies, ~35%
    under Announced Pledges, >40% under Net Zero (fact, IEA / S&P Global, 2025). Refined demand
    projected to ~33 Mt by 2035 (StPS). Data-center copper use alone 0.25–0.55 Mt/yr by 2030
    (fact, IEA). Driver: falling ore grades + long mine lead times.
  • Rare-earth concentration: China ~270,000 t REO mine production vs US 51,000, Australia
    29,000, Myanmar 22,000 — ~69% of world total (fact, USGS Mineral Commodity Summaries 2026).
    China = ~90% of refined/separated supply (fact, USGS). US imports of REE compounds/metals:
    71% from China (fact, USGS 2026).
  • Heavy REE (Dy/Tb): lynas-rare-earths is the only separated-heavy-REE producer outside
    China     (fact, 2025) — a structural choke point for high-temp NdFeB magnets.
  • Gallium/germanium: full Chinese export cut estimated to cut US GDP by ~$3.4B (range
    $1.7–9.0B) if simultaneous (fact, USGS OFR 2024-1057). China suspended its US export ban
    (gallium/germanium/antimony) until 27 Nov 2026 — temporary easing of Dec-2024 controls (fact,
    Fastmarkets / Mining.com, 2026) — i.e. supply is a policy switch, not a market.
  • Lithium: recovering — albemarle Energy Storage net sales +70% YoY (Q1'26) on +51% price
    / +14% volume (fact); signals the post-glut price recovery.

3. Key structural drivers

Demand side:
- Electrification + grid — copper is the binding input for transmission/distribution build-out
(energy-transition).
- AI data centers — incremental copper (power distribution, busways) + the whole power chain.
- EVs / energy storage — lithium + NdPr magnets.
- Defense / re-armament — magnets, gallium/germanium, antimony for munitions, optics, radar.

Supply side:
- Copper: declining ore grades, 10–15yr mine lead times, permitting, water — supply is slow.
- Rare earths / minor metals: policy-driven Chinese dominance (export controls as leverage);
Western capacity (MP, Lynas) nascent and government-backed (US DoW/DoD offtake at MP).
- Lithium: previously oversupplied; supply discipline + demand now tightening prices.

4. Value-chain / linkage

  • → energy-transition / ai-infrastructure: copper is the physical electron-carrier behind
    grids, data centers, EVs — the most direct overlap.
  • → semiconductors: gallium/germanium feed compound semis, optics, advanced packaging;
    supply is a Chinese policy lever.
  • → defense / reshoring: rare-earth magnets + minor metals are dual-use; Western governments are
    subsidizing domestic supply (MP–US DoW agreement, ceased China sales July 2025).
  • Chain: mine → concentrate → smelt/refine/separate (the China-dominated choke point for REE) →
    magnets/cathodes/wire → end product (EV, grid, weapon, data center).

5. Competitive landscape

  • Copper: consolidated majors with irreplaceable orebodies (decades-long moats):
    freeport-mcmoran (Grasberg + Americas), southern-copper (lowest-cost, Peru/Mexico),
    diversified bhp / rio-tinto / glencore (copper + iron ore + trading). M&A heating up
    (BHP / Rio / Glencore circling) signals scarcity of tier-1 copper assets.
  • Lithium: albemarle is the integrated Western leader; cyclical, price-driven.
  • Rare earths: mp-materials (US mine-to-magnet, government-backed) and lynas-rare-earths
    (only ex-China heavy-REE separator) are the strategic Western champions — moats are policy +
    scarcity of non-China refining    , not low cost.
  • Minor metals (Ga/Ge): no clean public pure-plays; exposure is geopolitical/embedded.
  • Margins/moats sit with lowest-cost copper (Southern Copper) and the policy-protected REE
    separators.

7. Competitiveness read

Copper is the highest-conviction sub-theme: the IEA's ~30% deficit-by-2035 (StPS) is a
supply-side problem with no fast fix — ore grades fall, mines take 10–15 years, and the M&A scramble
(BHP / rio-tinto / glencore) confirms how scarce tier-1 orebodies are. southern-copper
(lowest-cost, long-life) and freeport-mcmoran (tier-1 Grasberg, high price-leverage — $881M Q1
net income at $5.78/lb) are the cleanest pure-plays; the diversified majors (bhp, rio-tinto)
give copper growth funded by iron-ore cash but dilute the copper signal. The catch is these are
price-takers: the thesis is a bet on the copper price, and the equities have already run hard
(FCX +~100% in a year).

Rare earths are a different animal — the moat is policy and the scarcity of non-China
refining/separation, not cost. lynas-rare-earths (the only ex-China separated heavy-REE
producer; H1 FY26 revenue +63%, profit A$80.2M) and mp-materials (US mine-to-magnet, with DoW
price-protection that stabilizes earnings and an explicit ceasing of China sales) are strategic
assets whose value is underwritten by Western governments wanting supply-chain independence. But they
remain small, partly subsidy-dependent, and exposed to the policy switch: China's temporary lift
of the gallium/germanium/antimony ban (to Nov 2026) shows Beijing can turn Western pricing on and off.
Lithium (albemarle, +70% Energy Storage sales) is an earlier-cycle recovery play off the glut.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Copper price reversal — recession / China demand shock; equities are price-takers and have
    re-rated up sharply.
  • Rare-earth policy whipsaw — China easing exports (as in 2026) collapses Western pricing and
    undermines MP/Lynas economics; or substitution / thrifting reduces magnet REE intensity.
  • Subsidy dependence — MP's earnings lean on the DoW price-protection agreement; policy change is
    a single point of failure.
  • Lithium oversupply 2.0 — supply could outrun the recovery again.
  • Demand disappointment — slower EV/grid/AI build cuts the demand pull.
  • Project execution / permitting / resource nationalism in mining jurisdictions.
  • Data quality: several Q-level revenue/margin and net-cash fields NRFF this pass (diversified
    miners report half-yearly).

10. Conviction, stage, horizon

  • Conviction: 4/5 (inference) for copper (structural deficit, slow supply, M&A-confirmed
    scarcity); 3/5 for rare earths / minor metals (real strategic scarcity but policy-whipsaw
    and subsidy dependence cap conviction); 3/5 lithium (cyclical recovery). Blended ~3.5, held
    below 4 overall by price-taker dynamics + re-rated equities.
  • Stage: copper accelerating; rare earths emerging→accelerating; lithium emerging recovery.
  • Horizon: 3–10 years (mine lead times make this the longest-dated of the three themes).
  • Invalidation: copper price sustained breakdown AND/OR China fully re-opens REE/minor-metal
    exports (collapsing Western pricing) → step copper to 3, rare earths to 2.
