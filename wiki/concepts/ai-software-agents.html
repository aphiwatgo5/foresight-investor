AI Software & Agents

Definition: The application and agent layer of the AI stack — software where AI/LLMs are
the product or a core feature, sitting above compute/foundation-model infrastructure. Spans
LLM/AI platforms embedded in incumbent SaaS (Microsoft Copilot, Salesforce Agentforce, ServiceNow
AI), AI-native SaaS (Palantir AIP), and agentic software (autonomous multi-step "AI agents").
The monetization endpoint of ai-infrastructure. Conviction 4 on the trend-radar.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

The layer above GPUs/foundation models: (a) AI embedded in incumbent SaaS seats, (b) AI-native
SaaS, (c) agentic software that plans and executes tasks across enterprise apps. Excludes pure
infrastructure (ai-infrastructure, semiconductors).

3. Key structural drivers

Demand side: enterprise productivity/automation mandate; labor-cost substitution; the
40%-by-2026 agent-embedding wave (Gartner); data-gravity pulling AI workloads to where enterprise
data lives (Snowflake, Databricks, Datadog). Supply side: falling inference cost per token;
foundation-model commoditization pushing value to the application/orchestration layer; incumbents
bundling AI into existing seats (Microsoft 365 Copilot, Agentforce) to monetize without new sales
motion.

4. Value-chain / linkage

Foundation models (OpenAI/Anthropic/Google) → cloud compute & GPUs (ai-infrastructure,
semiconductors) → AI software/agents (this theme) → enterprise workflows. App-layer demand
pulls inference compute; conversely infra cost deflation expands app-layer margins. Cross-link to
cybersecurity: AI agents create a new attack surface and a new identity class ("agent
identities") — driving identity-security demand (the Palo Alto–CyberArk rationale).

5. Competitive landscape

Two camps: incumbent platforms (Microsoft, Salesforce, ServiceNow, Adobe, Snowflake) embedding
AI to defend/expand existing seats — moats from data, distribution, switching costs; and
AI-native / disruptors (Palantir, agent startups) winning greenfield workloads. Consolidation
is via bundling rather than mega-M&A. Margins highest where data + distribution coincide (Microsoft;
Adobe ~38% op margin; Palantir ~60% adj. op margin). Key tension: whether AI commoditizes
seat-based SaaS (the "is Salesforce/Adobe disrupted?" debate — note 2026 underperformance of
CRM/ADBE/NOW vs AI-natives).

7. Competitiveness read

Winning: AI-native and data-gravity names — Palantir (+85% rev, 60% adj op margin, US
commercial inflection) and Snowflake/Datadog (30%+ growth, AI-workload pull) take the
narrative and the multiple. Microsoft remains the structural winner on distribution: it can
attach AI to billions of existing seats at near-zero CAC. Pressured: seat-based incumbents
whose value-add is the UI layer AI may erode — Adobe and Salesforce both de-rated in 2026
on "is the moat AI-proof?" fears; both still grow 18–20% with fat margins, so the debate is
multiple, not fundamentals. The decisive question: does agentic AI expand incumbents' TAM (more
automation = more usage) or collapse it (one agent replaces many seats)? Evidence is mixed;
Gartner's ROI-gap warning favors caution.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Valuation extremes: Palantir at ~49x sales is priced for multi-year flawless execution
    (inference); any deceleration de-rates hard.
  • AI disruption of incumbent SaaS: if agents collapse seat counts, CRM/ADBE/NOW TAMs shrink —
    the core bear case.
  • ROI gap / "agent winter": Gartner (2026-05-05) — deployments not yet delivering measurable
    returns; risk of a budget pullback if 2026–27 pilots disappoint.
  • Foundation-model commoditization could help (cheaper inference) or hurt (app layer becomes a
    thin wrapper over commodity models).
  • Correlated AI-cyclical de-rating with ai-infrastructure/semiconductors — same engine.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 4/5 (calibrated — not 5: extreme valuations + unproven agent ROI cap risk/reward).
  • Stage: accelerating (40%-by-2026 agent embedding; spend +47% YoY).
  • Horizon: 3–7 years (multi-year secular; near-term de-rating risk high).
  • Invalidation: AI agent software spend growth decelerating below ~25% YoY in 2027, OR clear
    evidence agents collapse rather than expand incumbent seat counts, OR a broad "agent winter."
