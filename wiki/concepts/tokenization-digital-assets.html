Tokenization & Digital Assets

Definition: Three intertwined sub-themes: (a) stablecoins under the new US GENIUS Act framework;
(b) Bitcoin/crypto as an institutional asset class post-spot-ETF (IBIT) plus crypto-exposed equities
(COIN, MSTR, CRCL); (c) real-world-asset (RWA) tokenization (on-chain Treasuries/funds — BlackRock
BUIDL). The unifying thesis: traditional finance migrating onto blockchain settlement rails. Conviction
3 on the trend-radar; crypto was in a ~45% drawdown entering mid-2026 — read metrics as cyclical.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

(a) Stablecoins — fiat-pegged tokens now under a US federal charter regime (GENIUS Act): Circle
(USDC, compliant leader), Tether (USDT, larger but offshore); (b) BTC/crypto asset class — spot ETFs
(IBIT), corporate-treasury proxies (MSTR/Strategy), exchanges/custody (Coinbase); (c) RWA
tokenization — BlackRock (BUIDL), Franklin Templeton, Securitize (infra). Regulation is now the
primary moat-creator.

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: institutional allocation legitimized by spot ETFs (IBIT); corporate-treasury BTC adoption
(MSTR 818,334 BTC); stablecoins as dollar-access/payments rails (USDC on-chain volume +263% to $21.5T in
Q1'26); tokenized Treasuries offering on-chain yield (BUIDL). Supply: the GENIUS Act (enacted
2025-07-18) created a federal stablecoin charter — OCC conditionally granted national trust-bank charters
to Circle, Paxos + others (Dec 2025); BlackRock/JPMorgan/Franklin building tokenization infra; Securitize
providing issuance plumbing.

4. Value-chain / linkage

Issuers/protocols (Circle→USDC, Tether→USDT; BlackRock+Securitize→BUIDL) mint reserve-backed tokens →
exchanges/custodians (Coinbase) provide on/off-ramps, trading, custody → ETF sponsors (BlackRock/IBIT)
wrap BTC for TradFi → treasury-proxy equities (MSTR) act as leveraged BTC beta → RWA platforms tokenize
off-chain assets. Monetizes via reserve income (Circle $694M Q1'26 rev, +20%), trading/custody fees
(Coinbase $1.41B Q1'26 rev), and management fees (IBIT, BUIDL). Direct disruption link to
fintech-payments (stablecoin settlement vs card rails).

5. Competitive landscape

Bifurcated by regulatory posture post-GENIUS. Stablecoins consolidating around regulated US issuers:
Circle (USDC) is the compliant institutional leader with a first-mover charter; Tether (USDT, larger but
offshore) faces a US-distribution question mark. Exchanges/custody dominated by Coinbase (compliant US
incumbent — regulatory moat). ETF distribution is BlackRock's to lose (IBIT scale + brand). RWA
is early, led by TradFi giants (BlackRock, Franklin) + infra (Securitize) — nascent moats. Treasury
proxies (MSTR) have no operating moat — pure financial engineering. Regulation is the moat-creator.

7. Competitiveness read

GENIUS split the field into "compliant winners" and "regulatory question marks." Circle is the
cleanest stablecoin pure-play — a regulated USDC issuer with a national trust charter, surging on-chain
volume, and reserve-income economics that scale with rates/float; its risk is rate sensitivity (income
falls if rates drop) and Tether/bank competition. Coinbase is the compliant US gateway with a genuine
regulatory moat, but revenue is highly cyclical (Q1'26 GAAP loss during the drawdown). BlackRock is
the lowest-risk way to own institutional adoption (IBIT >$100B, BUIDL lead) — but crypto is immaterial to
BLK earnings, diluting the thematic exposure. MSTR is leveraged BTC beta with no operating moat; its
$12.5B Q1'26 mark-to-market loss and first BTC sale since 2022 are sentiment flags. RWA tokenization
is the most promising long term but smallest today (~$32B) with the widest forecast dispersion.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Regulatory execution — GENIUS final rules still pending; reserve/redemption/foreign-issuer
    treatment unsettled.
  • Crypto price cyclicality — ~45% drawdown from Oct'25 peak directly hits COIN, MSTR, IBIT fees.
  • Stablecoin rate sensitivity — Circle reserve income falls if rates decline; depeg/run risk.
  • MSTR leverage/refinancing — model depends on issuing at premiums; BTC sale may signal stress.
  • RWA adoption uncertainty — order-of-magnitude forecast dispersion; could lag badly.
  • Counterparty/custody/smart-contract risk; Tether opacity.

9. Investable instruments (candidates — not recommendations)

  • IBIT (BlackRock spot-BTC), FBTC (Fidelity), spot-ETH ETFs, crypto-equity ETFs (BITQ, BLOK). (Verify AUM/ER.)
  • Representative securities: CRCL (stablecoin pure-play), COIN (compliant exchange), MSTR (leveraged BTC
    proxy), BLK/BEN (diversified TradFi-on-chain). Direct BTC/ETH for asset-class exposure.

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 3/5 — a structural TradFi-onto-blockchain trend with the safest wrapper (IBIT/BlackRock)
    and a genuine post-GENIUS stablecoin inflection (Circle), but capped by crypto cyclicality, rate
    sensitivity, and the speculative/early state of RWA. Sub-scores: institutional/stablecoin 3; RWA 2
    (high optionality, early); crypto-proxy equities 2 (volatile).
  • Stage: accelerating (post-GENIUS). Horizon: 3–10 years.
  • Invalidation: adverse final GENIUS rules / regulatory reversal, OR sustained ETF outflows, OR
    RWA adoption stalling below ~$100B by 2028.
