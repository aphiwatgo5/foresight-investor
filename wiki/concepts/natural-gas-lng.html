Natural Gas & LNG

Definition: The thesis that natural gas is the near-term bridge fuel for the AI-power
buildout and global energy demand — spanning upstream gas production, US LNG export capacity,
gas turbines (the only dispatchable generation buildable at scale this decade), and the
pipeline / midstream that moves the molecule. Insight only — not advice (trend-radar).

1. Definition & scope

Four linked sub-segments:
- Upstream gas — Appalachian / Permian / Haynesville producers (e.g. eqt).
- LNG export / liquefaction — terminal operators turning US gas into seaborne cargoes
(cheniere-energy, venture-global).
- Gas turbines — heavy-duty turbine OEMs supplying new combined-cycle / peaker capacity
(ge-vernova, siemens-energy) plus turbomachinery for LNG (baker-hughes).
- Midstream / pipelines — gathering, transport, storage feeding both domestic power and LNG
feedgas (williams-companies, kinder-morgan).

2. Market size & growth

  • US LNG exports: forecast to average 17.0 Bcf/d in 2026, +1.9 Bcf/d YoY; current peak
    export capacity 18.3 Bcf/d (fact, EIA Today in Energy / STEO, 2026). New 2026 startups:
    Golden Pass trains 1–2 (1.4 Bcf/d), Corpus Christi Stage 3 trains 5–7 (0.6 Bcf/d).
  • US gas exports forecast to grow ~30% by 2027 as LNG ramps (fact, EIA, 2026).
  • North America LNG export capacity could more than double by 2029 (fact, EIA, 2025).
  • Global LNG capacity: ~300 bcm/yr of new export capacity (a record) set to be added by
    2030, led by US + Qatar (fact, IEA Coming surge in LNG production, Oct 2025).
  • Global gas demand: +~1.5%/yr 2024–2030 (~+380 bcm) (fact, IEA Gas 2025). Shell sees global
    LNG demand +~60% by 2040 (est., Shell LNG Outlook 2025).
  • Gas turbines: OEMs sold out into ~2028–2029; only three players can build large units —
    ge-vernova, siemens-energy, Mitsubishi Heavy Industries (fact, trade press / OEM guidance,
    Q1 2026). GE Vernova gas-turbine backlog hit 100 GW in Q1 2026 (from 83 GW), +21 GW signed in
    the quarter (fact, GE Vernova Q1 2026 8-K / Utility Dive, Apr 2026).

3. Key structural drivers

Demand side:
- AI data-center power — gas is the only dispatchable generation buildable at scale this
decade; turbines bridge the gap until nuclear/grid catch up (ai-infrastructure,
energy-transition).
- LNG pull — Asia-Pacific = ~50% of gas-demand growth to 2030; Middle East ~30% (oil-to-gas
switching) (fact, IEA Gas 2025).
- Coal-to-gas switching + reshoring/industrial electrification.

Supply side:
- Turbine bottleneck — 5-year backlogs; GE Vernova guiding 2026 equipment priced 10–20 pts
higher $/kW vs Q4 2025 (fact, GE Vernova Q1 2026). Pricing power is the story.
- Feedgas — abundant low-cost Appalachian/Permian supply; EQT record FCF $1.8B Q1 2026 (fact).
- Midstream — record gas-transport volumes on LNG export pull (Kinder Morgan, Q1 2026).

4. Value-chain / linkage

  • → ai-infrastructure / energy-transition: turbines + gas-fired power are the literal
    electrons behind AI compute; this is the most direct "AI needs power" expression.
  • → semiconductors: indirect — fabs and data centers are the marginal power buyers.
  • → defense / energy security: US LNG as a geopolitical export tool (Europe supply).
  • Internal chain: producer (eqt) → pipeline (williams-companies, kinder-morgan) →
    liquefaction (cheniere-energy, venture-global, turbomachinery from baker-hughes) →
    seaborne export. Power leg: gas → turbine (ge-vernova, siemens-energy) → grid.

5. Competitive landscape

  • Turbines: oligopoly — 3 OEMs build ~two-thirds of new-plant turbines; deep moats (engineering,
    installed-base service annuities, multi-year backlog). Margins + pricing rising fastest here.
  • LNG export: concentrated, capital-intensive, long-term contracted (SPAs underpin financing);
    cheniere-energy is the US scale leader, venture-global the fast-growth disruptor.
  • Midstream: scale + irreplaceable pipeline networks (right-of-way moat); stable fee-based cash
    flows, high payout. williams-companies (gas-focused) and kinder-morgan dominate.
  • Upstream: most commoditized / price-taking; eqt differentiates on low-cost Appalachian
    scale + integration.

7. Competitiveness read

The turbine OEMs (ge-vernova, siemens-energy) are the highest-quality way to express the
AI-power-bridge thesis: a genuine three-player oligopoly, multi-year sold-out backlogs (100 GW at GE
Vernova), and visible pricing power (+10–20 pts $/kW into 2026) translating directly into margin
expansion (GE Vernova gas EBITDA margin +470 bps YoY). Backlog converts to revenue with high
visibility and rich installed-base service annuities behind it. The main caution is that valuations
have re-rated hard alongside the AI complex (correlated de-rating risk per trend-radar).

LNG export is a quasi-infrastructure cash machine — cheniere-energy raised FY26 EBITDA guide
to $7.25–7.75B on record cargo volumes, and the contracted SPA model decouples economics from
spot-price swings (the $3.5B Q1 net "loss" was a non-cash derivative mark, not operating weakness).
venture-global offers faster growth but carries execution + contract-dispute risk. Midstream
(williams-companies, kinder-morgan) is the steady fee-based leg riding record gas-transport
volumes. Upstream (eqt) is the highest-beta, most price-dependent expression.

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Global LNG oversupply glut (~300 bcm of capacity added by 2030) could crush margins / spreads
    late-decade (fact, IEA / IEEFA warnings, 2025) — invalidation watch.
  • AI-power demand disappoints / digestion — if data-center buildout slows, turbine + gas demand
    re-rates with it (ai-infrastructure circular-financing risk).
  • Faster nuclear / renewables + storage displaces gas as the bridge sooner than expected.
  • Commodity price collapse hits upstream (eqt) hardest; LNG/midstream more insulated.
  • Policy / permitting reversal (LNG export authorizations, pipeline approvals).
  • Valuation — turbine + cooling-adjacent power names already richly priced.

10. Conviction, stage, horizon

  • Conviction: 4/5 (inference) — strongest, most visible piece is the turbine oligopoly (hard
    backlog + pricing power). LNG export is a durable cash-flow story. Held below 5 by late-decade LNG
    oversupply risk and correlation to the AI complex.
  • Stage: accelerating (turbines/LNG ramp); midstream more mature/steady.
  • Horizon: 3–7 years (turbine backlog visibility ~5y; LNG capacity to 2030).
  • Invalidation: AI-power demand stalls AND/OR 2028–2030 LNG glut compresses spreads → step down
    to 2–3.
