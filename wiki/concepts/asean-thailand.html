ASEAN & Thailand

Definition: Southeast Asia as a "China+1" manufacturing beneficiary and young-demographic
consumer story — Vietnam and Indonesia as the growth leaders, Singapore as the financial hub,
and Thailand as a special case (home market of this wiki's owner) that is structurally
challenged: a 2025 world-worst equity market, weak growth, and an aging population. Conviction 3
at the region level on the trend-radar; Thailand standalone ~2 — included for relevance,
flagged as the regional laggard.

Research/insight only — no buy/sell advice. Figures tagged (fact)/(est.)/(inference) with
period + source. "n/f" = no reliable figure found.

1. Definition & scope

(a) Vietnam — fastest grower, China+1 manufacturing magnet (Vingroup VIC, Vietcombank VCB);
(b) Indonesia — large domestic consumer + resources (Bank Central Asia BBCA, Bank Rakyat
BBRI); (c) Singapore — financial/REIT hub (DBS D05); (d) Thailand — energy/retail/
electronics (PTT, CP All CPALL, Delta Electronics DELTA) but a structurally weak market. The
region splits sharply between growth leaders (VN/ID) and the Thai laggard.

3. Key structural drivers

Demand: young, growing populations (esp. Vietnam, Indonesia, Philippines) driving consumption
+ financial deepening; rising middle class. Supply: "China+1" supply-chain relocation
(links to reshoring-supply-chain and semiconductors back-end/electronics — e.g. Thailand's
Delta Electronics) bringing manufacturing FDI; infrastructure investment. Thailand-specific
headwinds: aging demographics, high household debt, political instability, weak tourism recovery
and structural competitiveness erosion — why the SET was a global laggard.

4. Value-chain / linkage

Manufacturing FDI ("China+1") → export factories (VN/ID/Thailand) → domestic banks
(VCB/BBCA/BBRI/DBS) fund growth → consumers. Thailand's Delta Electronics ties to AI/electronics
supply chains (semiconductors, ai-infrastructure power components). Region links to
reshoring-supply-chain (China+1), india-em-growth (EM-growth peer/competitor for FDI),
and macro-regime (EM-flow/USD sensitivity). Thailand is also where fintech-payments
(PromptPay) and the owner's domestic context sit.

5. Competitive landscape

Country-by-country bank oligopolies + select champions: Indonesia's BBCA/BBRI (high-ROE, deep
deposit franchises — among EM's best banks); Vietnam's VCB (state-linked leader) + Vingroup
(conglomerate); Singapore's DBS (regional financial hub, high dividend); Thailand's PTT (energy
SOE), CP All (retail), Delta (electronics — the rare Thai growth/AI-supply name). Moats:
deposit/distribution scale (banks), SOE/licensing (energy), and global-supply-chain integration
(Delta). Thailand's broad market lacks structural growth drivers.

7. Competitiveness read

ASEAN is a two-speed region. The growth leaders — Vietnam (~6.3% GDP, the prime China+1
manufacturing magnet) and Indonesia (~4.7%, huge domestic consumer base with best-in-class
banks BBCA/BBRI) — offer genuine EM structural growth, with Singapore (DBS) as the
high-quality, high-dividend financial anchor. Thailand, by contrast, is the structural
laggard: the SET was among the world's worst-performing markets (~−16% in 1Q25) on aging
demographics, high household debt, political instability, and weak competitiveness — GDP growth
of only ~1.3–2.0% offers little earnings tailwind. The Thai bright spots are stock-specific —
Delta Electronics (AI/datacenter power components) is the rare growth/quality name riding the
global electronics-supply boom. Net: the region warrants a 3 on VN/ID/Singapore quality;
Thailand standalone is a ~2 — a market to be selective in (Delta), not own broadly. (Flagged
for relevance: this is the wiki owner's home market.)

8. Risks / what kills the thesis

  • Thailand structural decline — demographics, debt, politics, competitiveness erosion.
  • EM-flow/USD sensitivity — small, liquid-constrained markets whipsaw on global risk
    (macro-regime).
  • Currency volatility (THB, IDR, VND) eroding USD returns.
  • Vietnam market-access/upgrade uncertainty (frontier→EM reclassification timing).
  • China+1 underdelivery — FDI relocation could lag the narrative (reshoring-supply-chain).
  • Political instability (Thailand) and governance/transparency (frontier markets).

10. Our view — conviction / stage / horizon

  • Conviction 3/5 (region) — Vietnam/Indonesia offer real China+1 + demographic growth and
    Singapore/Indonesia have top-tier banks, but small market sizes, FX, and liquidity constraints
    cap the regional score. Thailand standalone ~2 — a structurally challenged market best
    approached selectively (Delta), included here for owner relevance.
  • Stage: emerging/mixed (VN/ID accelerating; Thailand stagnant). Horizon: 5–10 years.
  • Invalidation (region): China+1 FDI fails to materialize + EM-flow drought; (Thailand) no
    resolution of the debt/demographic/competitiveness drag.
